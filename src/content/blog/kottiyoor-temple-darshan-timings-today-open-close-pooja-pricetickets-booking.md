Kottiyoor Temple Complete Guide to Darshan Timings, Pooja Rates, Booking & Accommodation 

**Nestled in the serene hills of Kerala’s Kannur district, the Kottiyoor Temple (Akare Kottiyoor) is a unique and ancient forest shrine dedicated to Lord Shiva.** Unlike typical Keralan temples, this hallowed site is open only during a specific 27-day annual festival, making a visit here a rare spiritual opportunity. If you are planning a pilgrimage to this powerful  _Shakti Peetham_ in 2025, this comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know: from exact pooja rates and darshan timings to booking procedures and comfortable stays.

## Why Kottiyoor Temple is a Sacred Must-Visit

Believed to be the location where Goddess Sati’s  _Aksha_ (eye) fell, Kottiyoor holds immense significance as a **Shakti Peetha**. The temple complex comprises two main sites:

  * **Akare Kottiyoor (Ikkare Kottiyoor):** The main, permanent temple on the eastern bank of the Bavali River.
  * **Vadakke Kottiyoor (Umare Kottiyoor):** The temporary, bamboo-and-wood structure erected on the western bank only during the **Vaisakha Mahotsavam** (May-June). This is the primary venue for the legendary ritual of **‘Ellum Thudiya Dinam’** (The Day of Seven Knocks).

The raw, natural setting—with rituals performed by tribal communities—offers a deeply authentic and primal spiritual experience, drawing thousands of devotees each season.

## Kottiyoor Temple Darshan Timings 

The temple is **open only during the annual festival period** , typically from mid-May to early June. For 2025, the expected dates are **from May 18 to June 14** (subject to official confirmation based on the Malayalam calendar).

### Daily Schedule During Festival Season

**Ritual / Event**| **Morning Session**| **Evening Session**  
---|---|---  
**Temple Opens**|  5:00 AM| 5:00 PM  
**Nada Open (Darshan Starts)**|  5:30 AM| 6:30 PM  
**Main Poojas (Usha Pooja, etc.)**|  7:00 AM onwards| 7:00 PM onwards  
**Temple Closes**|  1:00 PM| 9:00 PM (After Athazha Pooja)  
  
> **Important Note:** The temple remains closed between the morning and evening sessions. **“Ellum Thudiya Dinam”** , the most important day, usually falls in early June. On this day, timings change drastically, with special rituals and massive crowds. Plan to arrive very early.

## How to Book Poojas Online at Kottiyoor Temple

To ensure your participation in rituals, **advance online booking is highly recommended** , especially for major Homams.

### Step-by-Step Booking Guide:

  1. **Visit the Official Portal:** Go to the **[Kerala Police’s ‘Temple Worship’ booking website](https://templeworship.keralapolice.gov.in/)**.  _This is the authorized platform for Kottiyoor bookings._
  2. **Register/Login:** Create an account with your mobile number and email.
  3. **Select Temple:** Find and select “Kottiyoor Temple (Vadakke)” from the list.
  4. **Choose Date & Pooja:** Select your pilgrimage date and the pooja you wish to book from the available list.
  5. **Make Payment:** Pay securely online via net banking, UPI, or card.
  6. **Get Confirmation:** Your e-receipt and booking confirmation will be sent via email and SMS. **Keep this safe for entry.**

> **Pro Tip:** Bookings usually open 2-4 weeks before the festival starts. Mark your calendar and book early, as slots for popular poojas like Mrityunjaya Homam sell out fast.

## Finding the Best Accommodation: Where to Stay Near Kottiyoor Temple

Since the temple is in a remote, forested area, planning your stay is crucial for a comfortable pilgrimage.

### **Options Near the Temple (Walking Distance)**

  * **Devaswom Guest Houses:** The **Malikappuram Guest House** and other Devaswom facilities are the most convenient. They are basic, clean, and affordable. **Booking opens with pouja bookings on the same portal.**
  * **Private Lodges/Homestays:** Limited options like **Kottiyoor Gardens** offer simple rooms. Book months in advance.

### **Options in Nearby Towns (20-45 mins drive)**

For more amenities, consider staying in these towns and commuting by taxi/auto.

  * **Kannur City:** Wide range of hotels, from budget to luxury (e.g., **Mascot Beach Resort** , **Broad Bean**).
  * **Thalassery:** Good mid-range hotels and famous for its cuisine.
  * **Iritty & Payyanur:** Closer towns with basic hotels and good connectivity.

**Our Recommendation:** For first-time visitors, try to secure a **Devaswom guest house** for ultimate convenience. Families or those seeking comfort should book a hotel in **Kannur** and hire a day taxi.

## Essential Pilgrim Tips for a Smooth Visit

  * **Dress Code:** Traditional attire is preferred. Men: Dhoti or Mundu (no shirt required for sacred bath areas). Women: Saree, Salwar Kameez, or Mundum Neriyathum.
  * **Footwear:** Leave footwear at the designated racks before entering the temple precincts.
  * **The River Crossing:** To reach Vadakke Kottiyoor, you must wade through the shallow **Bavali River**. Wear appropriate, quick-dry clothing and be cautious on the rocky riverbed.
  * **Crowds & Safety:** “Ellum Thudiya Dinam” sees unprecedented crowds. Follow police instructions, avoid rushing, and keep valuables secure.
  * **Health:** Carry water, basic medicines, and an umbrella or raincoat (weather can be unpredictable).

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q1: What are the exact 2025 festival dates for Kottiyoor Temple?**  
A: While based on the lunar calendar, the **Vaisakha Mahotsavam** for 2025 is **tentatively from May 18 to June 14**. The final dates will be confirmed by the temple committee in early 2025.

**Q2: Can I do darshan without booking a pooja?**  
A: **Yes.** General darshan is free and open to all during temple hours. However, booking a pooja guarantees a more structured participation and sometimes a priority line.

**Q3: Is there an offline booking option?**  
A: Yes, a limited number of pooja tickets are available at the temple counter, but they sell out within minutes. **Online booking is the only reliable method.**

**Q4: What is special about ‘Ellum Thudiya Dinam’?**  
A: It is the climactic day of the festival, symbolizing the moment Lord Shiva struck the ground seven times with his staff. A powerful, all-night ritual takes place, and it’s believed that the goddess’s presence is most potent then.

**Q5: How to reach Kottiyoor Temple?**  
A: **Nearest Airport:** Kannur International Airport (70 km). **Nearest Railway Station:** Kannur (55 km) or Thalassery (48 km). From these points, hire a taxi or take a bus to Kottiyoor.
