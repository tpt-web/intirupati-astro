On the night of February 1st, 2026, the sacred hills of Tirumala will come alive with one of its most spectacular monthly events—the **Magha Pournami Garuda Seva**. This celestial procession is a profound opportunity for devotees to witness Lord Malayappa Swami in magnificent splendor.

## The Grand Procession: A Divine Sight

On the auspicious full moon day (_Pournami_), the processional deity of **Sri Malayappa Swami** is exquisitely adorned with rare diamond armor, a majestic crown, and precious ornaments. The Lord is then mounted upon the resplendent **Golden Garuda Vahanam** , His divine vehicle.

The grand procession will travel along the **four Mada streets** of Tirumala, blessing all devotees who gather. It is a vibrant spectacle where servants hold traditional  _chatra_ (parasol) and  _chamara_ (fly whisk), accompanied by elephants, horses, and oxen.

  * **Event:** Monthly Pournami Garuda Seva (special for Magha Pournami)
  * **Date:** February 1, 2026
  * **Time:** 7:00 PM to 9:00 PM
  * **Location:** Four Mada Streets, Tirumala

## The Significance of Garuda Seva

This seva holds deep spiritual importance. Lord Garuda, the mighty eagle, is the eternal vehicle (_vahanam_) of Lord Venkateswara. He is revered for having brought the Venkatadri Hill (Tirumala) to its present sacred location. The annual Brahmotsavam itself begins with the hoisting of a flag bearing the Garuda emblem.

A special highlight is the **Sahasranama Kasula Haram** , a rare and breathtaking necklace of gold coins, which adorns the Lord during this procession. These precious ornaments, typically housed within the sanctum sanctorum, are brought out only for such special occasions, making the sight of the deity truly unforgettable.

The Magha Pournami Garuda Seva is more than a ritual; it is a direct and magnificent blessing. As the Lord rides forth on Garuda, devotees are filled with devotional ecstasy, making it a pinnacle experience for any pilgrimage to Tirumala.
