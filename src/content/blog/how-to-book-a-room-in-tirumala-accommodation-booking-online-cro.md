Planning your pilgrimage to Tirumala? **How to Get Rooms or Private Rooms in Tirumala** , Securing accommodation is a key part of your journey. This simple guide explains the two main ways to get a room: the **highly recommended online booking** system for a guaranteed stay, and the **walk-in process at the[Central Reception Office (CRO)](/blog/cro-office-tirumala-room-booking-process-status-timings/)** for last-minute arrangements. We cover official portals, timings, costs, and pro tips to ensure a smooth and comfortable visit to the sacred hills.

### Key Takeaways & Pilgrim Tips

  1. **Book Online If Possible** : This is the most important advice. Securing a room online 60 days ahead saves you from uncertainty and a potentially long, tiring wait at the CRO after your journey.
  2. **For Offline Booking: Be Prepared to Queue** : If you must use the CRO, plan to be at the office **by 5:00 AM or earlier**. Have your **original Photo ID (Aadhaar)** and **darshan ticket** ready. The waitlist often fills by mid-morning.
  3. **Understand the Allotment Rules** : Accommodation is generally for groups/families. **Single occupancy is typically not allowed** for basic CRO-allotted rooms.
  4. **Check Your SMS** : If you register offline, you will receive an SMS when a room is allotted. You usually have **about 30 minutes** to report to the counter and collect your key.

### Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

**Q1: What is the ARP counter, and is it the same as the CRO?**  
No, they are separate. The **ARP (Advance Reservation Portal) Counter** is where you collect keys for **pre-booked online rooms**. It is open 24 hours and is located near the CRO office. The **CRO counter** is only for **same-day, walk-in registration**.

**Q2: I have an online booking. Where do I go when I reach Tirumala?**  
Go directly to the **24-hour ARP Counter** (not the CRO queue). Show your booking confirmation and photo ID to collect your room key.

**Q3: What if the CRO waiting list is full for the day?**  
If the CRO stops accepting registrations, your options are to check for last-minute cancellations at the ARP counter (unlikely) or seek accommodation in **private lodges** outside the TTD complex.

**Q4: Is facial scanning mandatory for offline booking?**  
Yes, based on current procedures, a facial scan is part of the biometric registration process at the CRO to manage the waitlist efficiently.

By planning ahead and choosing the right booking method, you can ensure your stay in Tirumala is comfortable, allowing you to focus on your spiritual journey.
