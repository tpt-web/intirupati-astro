Planning to witness the beautiful Thomala Seva at Tirumala? This special flower decoration ritual is one of the most divine experiences you can have. Here’s your complete guide to booking Thomala Seva tickets online and everything you need to know before your visit.

## What is Thomala Seva?

**Thomala Seva** means “garland of flowers” in Telugu. This is a special ritual performed after the Suprabhatam Seva, where Lord Venkateswara is decorated with beautiful flower garlands and Tulasi (holy basil) decorations.

Also known as **Thodutha Malai** in Tamil, this seva happens early morning when the priests adorn the main deity (Mula Virat) with colorful flower garlands in a traditional procession with lighted torches and the rhythmic sound of Jeganta bells.

The flowers from Yamunathurai are brought by the Periya Jiyar (chief pontiff of Tirumala temple) or his disciple. These garlands are carefully arranged in different sizes to decorate specific parts of the deity, creating a stunning divine appearance.

## Why is Thomala Seva Special?

Attending Thomala Seva offers devotees a unique opportunity to:

  * **Witness Flower Decoration** : See Lord Venkateswara being adorned with fresh flower garlands
  * **Close Darshan** : Limited crowd ensures a peaceful and intimate darshan experience
  * **Traditional Ritual** : Be part of an ancient tradition that’s been followed for centuries
  * **Divine Atmosphere** : Experience the sacred early morning atmosphere with vedic chants
  * **Special Blessing** : Receive prasadam after the seva

## Thomala Seva Timings & Details

### Basic Information:

  * **Seva Days** : Only on Tuesday, Wednesday, and Thursday
  * **Reporting Time** : 3:00 AM at VQC-I
  * **Seva Time** : 3:30 AM to 4:00 AM
  * **Duration** : Approximately 30 minutes
  * **Ticket Price** : Rs. 220 per person
  * **Persons Per Ticket** : Only 1 person (no group booking)
  * **Prasadam** : 1 small laddu per person

**Important Note** : Thomala Seva tickets are not available on Mondays during May, June, and July. On other days of the week, it’s performed in Ekantham (without devotees).

## How to Book Thomala Seva Tickets Online

Thomala Seva tickets are available through the **Electronic Lucky DIP system** – same as Suprabhatam Seva. Follow these simple steps:

### Step-by-Step Booking Process:

#### Step 1: Visit TTD Website

Go to [ttdevasthanams.ap.gov.in/home/dashboard](https://ttdevasthanams.ap.gov.in/home/dashboard)

#### Step 2: Login

  * Enter your Indian mobile number
  * Verify with OTP
  * Login to your account (or register if you’re new)

#### Step 3: Select Seva Electronic DIP

  * Under “Pilgrim Services”, click on “Seva Electronic DIP”
  * Read all instructions carefully
  * Check the box to accept terms and conditions
  * Click “Continue”

#### Step 4: Fill Registration Details

  * Enter all required personal information
  * Upload valid ID proof
  * Select number of persons (Note: Thomala Seva allows only 1 person per booking)
  * Double-check all details before proceeding

#### Step 5: Choose Thomala Seva

  * You’ll see a list of available sevas under Electronic DIP
  * Click on “Select Seva & Date” button
  * Find “Thomala Seva” and select the checkbox
  * Choose your preferred dates (Tip: Select multiple dates to increase your chances)
  * Click “OK”

#### Step 6: Review & Submit

  * Click “Continue to Review”
  * Carefully verify all entered information
  * Click “Confirm & Submit”
  * Note down your registration number

#### Step 7: Wait for Lucky Draw Results

  * The lucky draw date will be announced
  * If selected, you’ll receive SMS and email notification
  * You must pay within 3 days of selection to confirm your booking

### Booking Tips for Better Chances:

  1. **Register for Multiple Dates** : Select all available dates for Thomala Seva to increase selection probability
  2. **Consider All Sevas** : Many devotees register for all available sevas (Suprabhatam, Thomala, etc.) to maximize chances
  3. **Complete Payment Quickly** : Once selected, pay immediately – don’t wait for the 3-day deadline
  4. **Keep Contact Details Updated** : Ensure your mobile number and email are correct
  5. **Save Registration Number** : Keep it safe for future reference

## What to Expect During Thomala Seva

When you attend Thomala Seva, here’s what happens:

  1. **Arrival** : Report at VQC-I by 3:00 AM with your printed ticket
  2. **Verification** : Show your ID proof and booking confirmation
  3. **Procession** : Watch the priests carry flower garlands in a traditional torch-lit procession
  4. **Flower Decoration** : Witness the beautiful flower decoration of Lord Venkateswara
  5. **Darshan** : Get a close view of the decorated deity
  6. **Prasadam** : Receive blessed laddu prasadam
  7. **Duration** : The entire experience takes about 30 minutes

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I book for 2 people in Thomala Seva?** No, Thomala Seva allows only 1 person per booking.

**Q: Is Thomala Seva performed daily?** No, only on Tuesday, Wednesday, and Thursday. Other days it’s performed in Ekantham (without devotees).

**Q: What if I’m not selected in the lucky draw?** You can register again in the next booking window or try booking other sevas.

**Q: Can I cancel my booking?** Once selected and paid, cancellations are not allowed. Please plan accordingly.

**Q: Are children allowed?** Yes, children need separate tickets. There’s no age limit.

**Q: How early should I reach Tirumala?** Reach Tirumala at least 3-4 hours before reporting time for smooth travel and parking.
