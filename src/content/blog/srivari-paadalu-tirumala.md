Srivari Padalu, also known as Srivari Mettu, is the most sacred and ancient footpath leading to the Tirumala hills. Translated as “The Sacred Feet of Lord Venkateswara,” these are the believed imprints of the Lord himself. For countless devotees, climbing this 11-km path is not just a trek, but a profound spiritual journey of surrender and devotion. 

**Srivari Paadalu, Tirumala – Timings, Darshan Ticket Counter, Location Distance, Festivals**

### **📍 What Are Srivari Padalu?**

Srivari Padalu refers to two sacred rock impressions on the ancient footpath (Alipiri Mettu) believed to be the footprints of Lord Venkateswara. These holy marks are located about **2 km uphill from the Alipiri toll gate**.

  * **Meaning:** The name literally means “Sri’s (Lord Venkateswara’s) footprints.”
  * **Significance:** Devotees touch or pour milk over these imprints as an act of reverence, seeking blessings for the arduous climb ahead.

### **🚶‍♂️ Two Paths to Reach the Footprints**

You can reach Srivari Padalu via two routes starting from **Alipiri** :

  1. **Traditional Footpath (Srivari Mettu):** The direct, steep staircase route. The footprints are located along this path.
  2. **TTD Footpath (Alternative Staircase):** A parallel, well-maintained path built by TTD. You can take this path and cross over to see the Padalu.

“Srivari Padalu” refers to the footpath treks (Alipiri & Srivari Mettu) to Tirumala, which don’t need advance paid tickets but require free Divya Darshan (SSD) tokens, available daily at specific locations (Bhudevi Complex, [Srinivasam](/blog/srinivasam-complex-accommodation-prices-booking-complete-guide/), [Vishnu Nivasam](/blog/vishnu-nivasam-accommodation-room-booking-prices-complete-details/)) on a first-come, first-served basis, with specific timings and step limits for each route, requiring Aadhaar and a photo capture for the tokens

### **🕒 Timings & Entry**

  * **Opening Time:** The footpaths open at **5:00 AM**.
  * **Closing Time:** The gates at Alipiri close at **7:00 PM**. No entry is permitted after this time for the ascent.
  * **Key Rule:** **You cannot start your climb after 7:00 PM.** Plan to begin your trek well before evening.
  * **Darshan:** Provides Divya Darshan (free for footpath pilgrims). 

### **📜 The Sacred Journey: Step-by-Step**

The full Alipiri Mettu (footpath) to Tirumala is approximately **11 km** (about 3550 steps). The journey is typically divided into zones:

  1. **From Alipiri to Srivari Padalu:** ~2 km. Your first major blessing point.
  2. **From Padalu to Gogarbham Dam:** Continue the ascent through shaded, scenic paths.
  3. **Final Stretch to Tirumala:** The last leg leading you to the holy town.

### **✅ Essential Pilgrim Tips & Facilities**

  * **Footwear:** Wear sturdy, comfortable shoes with good grip.
  * **Hydration:** Carry water. Free purified drinking water points are available along the route.
  * **Rest Stops:** There are several **TTD Kalyanamandapams** (rest houses) with basic facilities, toilets, and sheltered areas to rest.
  * **Luggage:** **Porter service (Doli/Pittu)** is available for hire at the base for those unable to carry bags.
  * **Safety:** Start early to avoid the afternoon sun. The path is well-lit at night.

### **🚫 Important Rules to Remember**

  * Plastic and alcohol are strictly prohibited on the path.
  * Maintain cleanliness; use dustbins provided.
  * Descending pilgrims have the right of way. Please step aside to let them pass.

### **🙏 Spiritual Significance**

Walking the Srivari Mettu is an act of **“Padiyatra”** —a penitent pilgrimage. Each step is an offering, a prayer, and a surrender to the Lord. The physical effort symbolizes the devotee’s dedication and is believed to cleanse the soul, making the final darshan of Lord Venkateswara even more rewarding.

  
Climbing the Srivari Mettu to witness the holy Srivari Padalu is a transformative experience that connects you to centuries of devotion. It’s a challenging yet deeply fulfilling way to complete your pilgrimage, ensuring your heart and spirit arrive in Tirumala fully prepared for the divine darshan.
