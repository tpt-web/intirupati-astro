Located in the peaceful town of **Melmalayanur** (also spelled Melmalaiyanur or Mel Malayanur) in Viluppuram District, Tamil Nadu, the **Sri Angala Parameswari Temple** (known as **Arulmigu Angalamman Temple** , **Angala Amman Temple** , or **Angala Parameshwari Amman Temple**) is a powerful Shakti peetham dedicated to Goddess **Angala Parameswari** (a fierce form of Goddess Parvati/Shakti). Situated about **32 km from Gingee** (Senji) and around **29 km from Tiruvannamalai** , this ancient temple is revered as a protector against evil forces, Kali Yuga challenges, and for granting boons like child blessings, obstacle removal, health, and prosperity. The Goddess faces north while blessing devotees—a unique feature—and the shrine includes an ancient snake pit (nagakannu) in the sanctum.

Devotees from across South India flock here, especially for the mystical **Mayana Kollai** festival, seeking the divine mother’s fierce yet compassionate grace in this guardian temple.

### What is Melmalayanur Angala Parameswari Temple Famous For?

This temple is renowned for its incarnation of Goddess Parvati as Angala Amman (born on a beehive/hive before Kali Yuga), the ancient snake pit in the inner sanctum, and the intense **Mayana Kollai** festival (cremation ground ritual) celebrated vibrantly in February-March. The Goddess is worshipped as **Kaval Deivam** (guardian deity) who protects from black magic, doshas, and worldly troubles. Special prayers here are believed to fulfill wishes, cure ailments, and provide strength in difficult times.

### Legends & History of Angala Parameswari Temple Melmalayanur

The core legend ties to Brahma’s fifth head and Brahma Hatya dosha:

Goddess Saraswati cursed Lord Shiva and Parvati after Shiva severed Brahma’s fifth head (which stuck to Shiva’s hand due to pride). Shiva wandered as a beggar with the skull, hungry and sleepless, while Parvati was cursed to ugly form with demonic attendants. To redeem, Parvati incarnated as a serpent (or on a beehive) in Melmalayanur, bathed in the holy tank to wash sins, and regained glory. She fed food balls to the skull (Brahma’s kapalam), causing it to detach from Shiva’s hand after the third ball—relieving the dosha. In fierce rakshasa form, she pressed the heads into the earth (celebrated as Mayana Kollai). Since then, she resides here as Angala Parameswari, facing north, blessing devotees eternally.

The temple area was part of ancient **Dandakaranya** (Tandakarunyam forest), with roots pre-Kali Yuga. It’s managed under HRCE Tamil Nadu, with daily poojas continuing traditions.

### Deity Details & Temple Highlights

  * **Presiding Deity (Amman)** : Sri Angala Parameswari / Angalamman / Angala Amman (fierce yet protective form of Parvati, facing north).
  * **Key Features** : Ancient snake pit in sanctum; temple tank for holy dips (believed to remove sins/doshas); vibrant architecture with gopuram and mandapams.
  * **Associated Deities** : Often linked to Shiva aspects; devotees perform Naga Pooja, etc., for dosha nivarthi.

### Festivals & Pooja Timings at Melmalayanur Angala Parameswari Temple

The temple buzzes with energy during festivals. Key celebrations:

  * **Mayana Kollai** (famous cremation ground festival, one day before full moon after Maha Shivaratri, Feb-March) – Energetic with trance, food offerings to cremation ground, and massive crowds.
  * **Navarathri Brahmotsavam** (10 days in September-October)
  * **Amavasya** (new moon days – special rituals, extended hours)
  * Regular Fridays and full moon days for special poojas.

**Daily Timings** (as per official HRCE):

  * Morning: 6:00 AM – 12:00 PM (or up to 12:30 PM in some sources)
  * Evening: 4:30 PM – 9:00 PM (open till late on Amavasya) Daily four kaala poojas; abhishekam, archanai, and special seva available.

  * **By Air** : Chennai International Airport (~129-150 km).
  * **Local Transport** : Frequent buses from Gingee, Tindivanam, or Arani; taxis/cabs easily available. PIN: 604204; Address: Melmalayanur, Kodukankuppam, Viluppuram District.
