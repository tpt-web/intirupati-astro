Tucked away on **Brahma Koil Street** in **Valayapettai Agraharam** , a serene part of **Kumbakonam** town in Thanjavur District, Tamil Nadu, the **Sri Varadaraja Perumal Temple** (also known as **Sri Varadaraja Perumal Kovil** or **Varadharaja Perumal Temple near Brahma Koil**) is a peaceful and revered Vaishnava shrine dedicated to Lord Vishnu as **Varadaraja Perumal** (the boon-granting king). This heritage temple, managed by the Saurashtra Dharma Paripalana Committee (a private trust), stands about **1.9 km from Kumbakonam Railway Station** and offers a tranquil spiritual atmosphere amid the temple-rich town famously called the “Temple Town of South India.”

Devotees visit for daily darshan, special poojas, and blessings of prosperity, health, and obstacle removal in this modest yet historically significant Perumal temple.

### What is Sri Varadaraja Perumal Temple Near Brahma Koil Famous For?

This temple is known for its over-century-old heritage (with major renovations in 1974 and 1998), control under the Saurashtra community trust since 1912, and its location on Brahma Koil Street—linking it to local legends of Lord Brahma. It provides a calm escape for Vaishnava devotees in Kumbakonam, with regular worship and festivals emphasizing Lord Vishnu’s grace as Varadaraja (bestower of boons).

### History & Inscriptions of Varadaraja Perumal Temple Kumbakonam

The temple is under the Saurashtra Dharma Paripalana Committee (established around 1912, celebrated 100 years in 2012). Key milestones include:

  * **Jeernotharana Ashta Bandhana Maha Samprokshanam** (major renovation consecration) on 3rd May 1974.
  * Another Maha Samprokshanam on 9th February 1998, with funds used for additional thirupani (temple maintenance).
  * Inscriptions and records highlight Saurashtra community patronage, community-led administration, and ongoing preservation of traditions.

While specific ancient legends are not prominently detailed (unlike major Divya Desams), its proximity to Brahma-related sites in Kumbakonam ties it to the town’s Puranic importance as a yagna sthalam of Lord Brahma.

### Deity Details & Temple Highlights

  * **Presiding Deity (Moolavar)** : Sri Varadaraja Perumal (Lord Vishnu in standing posture, granting boons).
  * **Consort (Thayar)** : Likely Perundevi Thayar or similar (common in Varadaraja shrines; confirm locally).
  * **Key Features** : Modest architecture typical of local agraharam temples; sanctum with Perumal; associated mandapams for poojas. The temple maintains a clean, peaceful environment ideal for meditation and prayer.

### Festivals & Pooja Timings at Sri Varadaraja Perumal Temple

Apart from daily rituals, special emphasis is on:

  * **Navaratri** (9 days, September-October)
  * **Vaikunta Ekadasi** (December-January, grand celebrations)
  * **Narasimha Jayanti** (April-May)
  * Other occasions like Rama Navami or community-specific events.

**Daily Timings** (approximate; verify locally as they may vary):

  * Morning: Typically 6:00 AM / 7:00 AM – 12:00 PM / 1:00 PM
  * Evening: 5:00 PM / 6:00 PM – 8:00 PM / 9:00 PM Regular poojas, abhishekam, archanai, and seva available; contact trust for special arrangements.

### How to Reach Sri Varadaraja Perumal Temple Near Brahma Koil Kumbakonam

  * **By Road** : Located in Valayapettai Agraharam on Brahma Koil Street; easily accessible within Kumbakonam town (auto-rickshaws, cycle rickshaws, or walk from main areas).
  * **By Rail** : Kumbakonam Railway Station (~1.9 km); frequent trains from Chennai, Trichy, Thanjavur.
  * **By Bus** : Kumbakonam Bus Stand (close by); TNSTC and private buses connect to major cities.
  * **From Major Cities** : ~290 km from Chennai, ~50 km from Thanjavur, ~100 km from Trichy.
  * **Local Tips** : Use local transport or Google Maps for Brahma Koil Street; PIN: 612001. Contact Saurashtra Dharma Paripalana Committee or temple priest for guidance.
