## 🙏 What is Kalyanotsavam Seva?

Srivari Kalyanotsavam is one of the most beautiful and spiritually significant sevas performed at Tirumala Temple. This divine wedding ceremony celebrates the celestial marriage of Lord Sri Malayappa Swami (the processional deity of Lord Venkateswara) with His consorts Sridevi (Goddess Lakshmi) and Bhudevi (Goddess Padmavathi).

The elaborate wedding ritual is conducted according to ancient Vaikhanasa Agama scriptures in the sacred Kalyana Mandapam. A specially consecrated purohit (priest) performs the marriage ceremony, while an archaka assists with various rituals. The entire ceremony lasts approximately 3 hours and recreates the divine wedding with all traditional customs.

**Special Feature:** This seva allows devotees, especially newly married couples, to witness and participate in the Lord’s marriage ceremony, seeking blessings for a harmonious and prosperous married life.

## 🎭 The Kalyanotsavam Ceremony – What Happens

The Kalyanotsavam is conducted with elaborate rituals following Vaikhanasa Agama traditions. Here’s what you’ll witness:

### 1\. Panchamurthi Puja (Initial Worship)

The ceremony begins with worship of five main deities, establishing divine presence and seeking blessings for the sacred wedding to follow.

### 2\. Vedic Mantras & Family Pedigree

Priests chant sacred Vedic mantras while reciting the divine family lineage (gotra) of the Lord and His consorts, just as in traditional Hindu weddings.

### 3\. Tera (Screen) Removal – First Sight

The deities face each other with a decorative screen (tera) between them. At the auspicious moment (muhurtam), the screen is removed, allowing the bride and groom to see each other for the first time – a beautiful moment that symbolizes divine union.

### 4\. Homa (Sacred Fire Ritual)

Priests perform elaborate fire rituals with offerings to the sacred fire, invoking divine blessings and purifying the atmosphere for the marriage ceremony.

### 5\. Mangala Sutra Dharana (Sacred Necklace)

The most significant moment – the Lord adorns His consorts with the mangala sutra (sacred wedding necklace), symbolizing eternal commitment and divine matrimony.

### 6\. Talambralu (Showering Rice)

The bride and groom shower each other with rice mixed with turmeric and flowers, symbolizing prosperity, fertility, and happiness in married life.

### 7\. Garland Exchange

The ceremony concludes with the Lord and His consorts being adorned with beautiful floral garlands, marking the completion of the divine wedding.

**Duration:** The entire ceremony takes approximately 1 hour, with devotees seated throughout to witness every sacred moment.

## 📜 Historical Significance of Kalyanotsavam

The tradition of celebrating Kalyanotsavam at Tirumala has a rich history dating back to **1536 AD**.

The renowned scholar and devotee **Tallapaka Tirumala Ayyangar** (from the same family as the legendary saint-poet Annamacharya) first conceived the idea of celebrating the divine marriage festival of Lord Malayappa Swami. Inspired by divine vision and deep devotion, he organized the first Kalyana Utsavam.

Initially, this grand celebration was conducted over **five days** in the historic **Tirumamani Mandapam** within the main temple premises. The festival included elaborate decorations, musical performances, devotional singing, and various rituals celebrating different aspects of the divine wedding.

Over the centuries, while the duration has been shortened to make it accessible to more devotees daily, the sanctity and significance of the ritual remain unchanged. The same Vaikhanasa Agama procedures established nearly 500 years ago are followed meticulously even today.

**Did you know?** The Kalyana Mandapam where this seva is now performed daily was specially built to accommodate the growing number of devotees wishing to witness this divine ceremony. It stands as a testament to the enduring faith of millions of devotees over centuries.

## 👔 Strict Dress Code Requirements

Traditional attire is MANDATORY for Kalyanotsavam Seva. Entry will be denied for improper clothing.

### For Men (Grooms/Male Devotees):

  * **Mandatory:** White Dhoti or Pancha (traditional Indian lower garment)
  * Must **remove shirt and vest** during the entire seva ceremony
  * Upper body must be bare as per temple tradition
  * Can wear shirt/kurta while traveling, but must remove before entering seva area
  * Clean, well-maintained traditional attire shows respect to the deity

### For Women (Brides/Female Devotees):

  * **Preferred:** Saree with blouse (most traditional and auspicious)
  * **Alternative Options:**
    * Punjabi dress/Salwar Kameez with Dupatta
    * Churidar with Dupatta
    * Half Saree (traditional South Indian attire)
  * Knees, shoulders, and midriff must be **completely covered**
  * Dupatta must be worn at all times

### Strictly Prohibited:

  * ❌ Western clothing (jeans, T-shirts, shorts, skirts)
  * ❌ Sleeveless tops or dresses
  * ❌ Short kurtas or crop tops
  * ❌ Bermudas or three-fourth pants
  * ❌ Any revealing or casual attire

**⚠️ IMPORTANT WARNING**

Security personnel strictly enforce dress code. Improper attire will result in entry denial with NO REFUND of your Rs. 1,000 ticket. Plan accordingly!

## 💡 Essential Tips for Smooth Kalyanotsavam Experience

  * **Book Early:** Kalyanotsavam tickets sell out quickly, especially on weekends and auspicious dates. Book 2-3 months in advance.
  * **Arrive on Time:** Reporting time is 10:00 AM at Supadham. Arrive by 9:30 AM to avoid last-minute rush.
  * **Carry Documents:** Must bring printed ticket (hard copy mandatory) and original ID proofs matching booking details.
  * **Dress Appropriately:** Wear traditional attire from home. Don’t plan to change at Tirumala – it’s risky and time-consuming.
  * **Travel Light:** Carry minimal luggage. Large bags are not allowed inside temple premises.
  * **Children Policy:** Children below 12 years are allowed free without tickets, but they too must follow dress code.
  * **Photography Banned:** No cameras, video recording, or mobile photography allowed during seva. Keep phone on silent.
  * **Stay Seated:** During the 3-hour ceremony, devotees must remain seated. Use restroom before seva starts.
  * **Prohibited Days:** Kalyanotsavam is NOT performed during major festivals (Brahmotsavam, Pavitrotsavam), Pushpayagam, or on eclipse days.
  * **Weather Check:** Tirumala can be cool in early mornings. Carry a light shawl (traditional) if visiting in winter months.
  * **Accommodation:** Book TTD guesthouses or hotels in advance, especially if attending early morning seva.
  * **Food:** Have breakfast before reporting at 10 AM. Light snacks available after seva.
  * **No Refunds:** Tickets are non-refundable and non-transferable. Ensure your travel plans are confirmed before booking.
  * **Health Precautions:** If anyone in your group has mobility issues, inform staff in advance for assistance.
  * **Respect Rituals:** Maintain silence during ceremony, follow all instructions from priests and temple staff.

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

### Q: Can a father and son attend Kalyanotsavam together?

Yes, absolutely! Any 2 adults can attend together – it doesn’t have to be a married couple. Father-son, mother-daughter, friends, siblings – all combinations are allowed.

### Q: What if only one person shows up but I booked for 2?

No problem. If you’ve booked for 2 people but only 1 person can attend, single person entry is allowed. Your ticket remains valid.

### Q: How many days in advance should I book?

Ideally, book 2-3 months in advance. TTD opens booking slots 90 days prior. Popular dates (weekends, festival seasons) get filled within hours of opening.

### Q: Can unmarried couples attend Kalyanotsavam?

Yes, Kalyanotsavam is open to everyone – married couples, unmarried couples, families, friends, individuals. There’s no restriction based on marital status for the regular quota.

### Q: Is the big laddu (2kg) included in Rs.1000 ticket?

Yes! The ticket price of Rs. 1,000 includes 1 big laddu (2 kg), 2 small laddus, and vastra bahumanam. No additional charges for these items.

### Q: Can I book online for the newly married couple quota?

No, the newly married couple quota (20 tickets/day) is only available offline at CRO Tirumala. You must personally visit with your marriage documents.

### Q: What if I lose my ticket printout?

Always carry backup – save PDF on phone, email to yourself. If lost, you can show the digital copy along with your booking reference number and ID proof, but physical printout is strongly recommended.

### Q: Can pregnant women attend Kalyanotsavam?

Yes, but consider that the seva lasts 3 hours and requires sitting throughout. Consult your doctor before planning. Inform temple staff if you need any assistance.

### Q: Is live telecast available for this seva?

Yes! Kalyanotsavam is live-streamed on SVBC (Sri Venkateswara Bhakti Channel) during seva timings (10:30 AM – 1:30 PM). You can watch from anywhere in the world.

### Q: What is the cancellation policy?

TTD seva tickets are NON-REFUNDABLE and NON-TRANSFERABLE. Once booked, you cannot cancel or change the date. Plan carefully before booking.

### Q: Can NRI/foreign nationals book this seva?

Yes, but you need an Indian mobile number for OTP-based login. Use a friend/relative’s number or get a local SIM card. All other procedures remain the same.

### Q: How crowded is it during the seva?

The Kalyana Mandapam can accommodate several hundred devotees. It gets full on weekends and festival seasons, but everyone gets a seat. Arrive early for better seating positions.
