Have you ever wished for a seva that connects you directly to the devotion of millions? Parakamani Seva at Tirumala is a unique and sacred opportunity for devotees to volunteer in counting the holy Hundi offerings, participating in the temple’s divine economy.

**Experience the Blessings of Service**

  * **Direct Sacred Service** : Assist in the vital task of counting and accounting the offerings from the Srivari Hundi.
  * **Rare Volunteer Opportunity** : Become a ‘Parakamani Sevakulu’ and serve in the abode of Lord Venkateswara.
  * **Transparent & Organized**: Registrations are managed through the official TTD Srivari Seva portal for a fair process.

> **Note** : This is a specific volunteer seva requiring advance online registration. Slots are limited and announced periodically by TTD.

### ✅ **Eligibility & How to Join**

To maintain the sanctity and efficiency of the service, TTD has set clear guidelines for volunteers.

Eligibility Criteria| Details  
---|---  
**Age Limit**|  18 to 60 years  
**Health**|  Physically strong and mentally fit for sustained service  
**Religion**|  Must belong to the Hindu faith  
**Background**|  Must have no criminal record or pending cases  
**Service Nature**| **100% Voluntary** – No monetary payment is provided

### **Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)**

**What is Parakamani Seva?**  
It is a volunteer service where devotees assist in counting the monetary offerings (Hundi) received at the Tirumala temple.

**Who can register for this seva?**  
Generally, any devotee who registers through the official TTD Srivari Seva portal when the quota is open can apply. Specific eligibility details are provided during the announcement.

**Is there a fee to perform this seva?**  
No, this is a voluntary service. Registration is for allocating a duty slot.

**How often is registration opened?**  
TTD opens registration for future months periodically. It is not an always-available service, so watching for official announcements is key.

**Where is the official information announced?**  
The primary source is the **TTD website** (`tirumala.org`) and its official news portals.
