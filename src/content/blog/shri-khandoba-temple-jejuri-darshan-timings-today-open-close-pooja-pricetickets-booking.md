**Perched on a hill in Maharashtra, Shri Khandoba Temple in Jejuri is a powerful fortress of faith dedicated to Lord Shiva’s warrior avatar.** Known as the “God who fulfills wishes” (Sakam Bhakti), the temple draws millions, especially during the world-famous **Bhandara festival** , where the sky turns gold with turmeric. Our 2026 guide gives you the latest timings, booking info, and essential tips for a smooth pilgrimage.

## Entry Fees & Special Darshan Prices

  * **General Darshan:** **FREE**. Every devotee can join the queue for a standard darshan at no cost.
  * **Special/Quick Darshan Ticket:** Approximately **₹50 per person**. This pass lets you access a priority line.
  * **Important Note:** The temple **does not currently offer online booking or e-tickets for general entry or special darshan**. You must purchase any paid tickets at the counter on-site.

## The Unmissable Bhandara Festival (Somvati Amavasya)

The **Bhandara or “Golden Festival”** is Jejuri’s spectacular signature event. Devotees celebrate by tossing sacred turmeric powder (_Bhandara_) into the air, covering the entire hill in a vibrant yellow glow.

  * **When:** It occurs on **Somvati Amavasya** —a new moon day that falls on a Monday. The date changes yearly based on the lunar calendar.
  * **What to Expect:** Massive crowds (often over 500,000 people), immense devotional energy, and a breathtaking visual spectacle. Plan for significant travel delays and book accommodation months in advance if you wish to attend.

## Pilgrim Tips & Temple Guidelines

  1. **Dress Code:** Wear modest, traditional, or formal clothing out of respect. The temple authorities discourage shorts, mini-skirts, and sleeveless tops.
  2. **Footwear:** You **must remove footwear** before entering the temple complex. Use the paid storage facilities available.
  3. **Prohibited Items:** Do not carry **alcohol, tobacco, cameras, or any leather items** (like belts or wallets) into the sanctum.
  4. **The Climb:** The main Gad Kot temple requires climbing about 200-250 steps. Wear comfortable shoes you can easily remove.
  5. **Best Time to Visit:** The cool winter months (November to February) are ideal for climate. For festival vibes, target Champa Shashthi (Nov/Dec) or the Bhandara festival.

### Frequently Asked Questions (Most Devotees Asked)

**Q1: What is the best time to visit Jejuri for the turmeric (Bhandara) festival?**  
**A:** Plan your visit for **Somvati Amavasya**. Check the 2026 Hindu calendar for the exact date, as it is a lunar festival.

**Q2: Can I book my darshan or pooja online for Khandoba Temple?**  
**A: No, the temple currently does not have an online booking system for darshan or poojas.** All arrangements and ticket purchases happen at the temple counters.

**Q3: Is there any online facility or website for bookings?**  
**A:** There is no official online booking website. For accurate information, always **contact the temple trust directly**.

**Q4: What are the main attractions inside the temple complex?**  
**A:** Don’t miss the ancient **Kadepathar shrine** (a natural  _swayambhu_ linga), the fortress-like **Gad Kot** with its deepmalas (lamp pillars), and the sacred **Nandi statue** at the entrance.

**Q5: Who are the deities worshipped here?**  
**A:** The temple primarily worships **Lord Khandoba** (Shiva) along with his consorts **Mhalsa** (an avatar of Parvati) and **Banai** (an avatar of Ganga).
