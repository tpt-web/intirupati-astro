A unique day of divine celebrations in Tirumala featuring a ceremonial hunt and a sacred marriage procession.

Tirumala will witness the grand observance of two significant festivals on **Friday, January 16, 2026** —**Srivari Parveta Utsavam** and **Goda Parinayotsavam**. This day, which coincides with the **Kanuma festival** , is marked by vibrant processions and special rituals, leading to the cancellation of all paid sevas.

## 🕉️ The Day’s Celebrations: A Timeline of Events

The day is packed with devotional activities. Here is the complete schedule for devotees:

**Time**| **Event**| **Details**  
---|---|---  
**Early Morning**| **Goda Parinayotsavam Procession**|  Garlands of Goddess Andal are carried from the **Pedda Jeeyar Swamy Mutt** to the **Srivari Temple** in a procession along the four Mada streets.  
**5:30 AM – 6:30 AM**| |   
**Afternoon**| **Parveta Utsavam Begins**|  The processional deities of **Sri Malayappa Swamy** and **Sri Krishna Swamy** are taken to the **Paruveta Mandapam**.  
**1:00 PM**| |   
**Afternoon & Evening**| **Asthanam & Mock Hunt**| Ceremonial court (Asthanam) and the famous symbolic hunting programs are held at the mandapam.  
**Late Evening**| **Return Procession**|  The deities return to the main Srivari Temple.  
  
## 📜 Important Information for Visiting Devotees

In view of these major festivals, the Tirumala Tirupati Devasthanams (TTD) has announced important changes for the day:

**Planning Your Visit:** Since these popular paid sevas are cancelled, the day is ideal for witnessing the unique public festivals and participating in the **free darshan** queues. Be prepared for larger crowds due to the Kanuma festival.

**Cancelled Arjita Sevas (Paid Services):** All paid sevas at the Srivari Temple remain cancelled on **January 16, 2026**. This includes:

Kalyanotsavam

Unjal Seva

Arjita Brahmotsavam

Sahasra Deepalankara Seva
