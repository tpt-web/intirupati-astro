Sri Veera Venkata Satyanarayana Swamy Temple, revered as **Annavaram Temple** , majestically sits atop the sacred **Ratnagiri Hill** in East Godavari District, Andhra Pradesh. As the second most visited temple in the state after Tirupati, this divine abode attracts millions of devotees annually who seek blessings, perform the famous Satyanarayana Vratham, and experience the unique spiritual energy of worshipping the Divine Trinity in a single magnificent idol.

## Satyanarayana Vratham Complete Guide

The **Sri Satyanarayana Vratham** is the temple’s most famous ritual, performed by thousands daily.

### What is Satyanarayana Vratham?

A sacred Hindu ritual dedicated to Lord Vishnu as **Satyanarayana** (Lord of Truth), involving:

  * Recitation of five sacred stories (Vratham Kathas)
  * Special prasadam offering
  * Archana and abhishekam
  * Prasadam distribution

### Who Should Perform?

  * **Newly married couples** – blessed married life
  * **New homeowners** – during Griha Pravesh
  * **New business owners** – prosperity
  * **Seekers of relief** – health, financial, family issues
  * **Spiritual seekers** – liberation and divine grace

## Temple History & Mythology

### The Sacred Legend

From **Skanda Purana** and **Agni Purana** :

  * **Meru** (lord of mountains) and **Menaka** performed penance
  * Lord Vishnu blessed them with two sons: **Bhadra** and **Ratnakara**
  * **Bhadra** became **Bhadrachalam** (where Lord Rama settled)
  * **Ratnakara** desired to emulate his brother
  * Lord Vishnu agreed to settle on Ratnakara as **Veera Venkata Satyanarayana Swamy**
  * Ratnakara thus became sacred **Ratnagiri Hill**

### Modern History

**1891:** Raja Ramanarayana (Zamindar) had divine vision commanding temple construction

**1933-34:** Major reconstruction and expansion

**2011-12:** Extensive renovation while preserving traditional architecture

### The Unique Trinity Idol

**13-Feet Tall Cylindrical Idol:**

  * **Base:** Lord Brahma – The Creator
  * **Middle:** Lord Shiva (Rudra) – The Destroyer
  * **Top:** Lord Vishnu – The Preserver

This unified **Divine Trinity (Trimurti)** makes Annavaram unique, appealing to both Vaishnavites and Shaivites.

### Name Origin

“**Annavaram** ” means:

  1. **Anna** (Food) – Famous for generous food distribution
  2. **Varam** (Blessings) – Deity bestows abundant blessings

## Temple Architecture

### Chariot-Shaped Dravidian Design

**Four Wheels:** One at each corner representing:

  * Sun and Moon
  * Eternal wheels of time
  * Perpetual cosmic cycle

**Structure Symbolism:**

  * **Seven Lokas Below:** Seven lower worlds
  * **Seven Lokas Above:** Seven higher worlds
  * **Garbhalaya (Center):** Deity ruling entire universe

### Two-Floor Structure

**Ground Floor:**

  * **Yantra** (Sacred geometric diagram)
  * **Panchayatana** (Five deities): 
    * Ganapati (East)
    * Suryanarayana Swamy (South)
    * Bala Tripurasundari (West)
    * Maheshwara Swamy (North)
    * Satyanarayana Swamy (Center)

**First Floor:**

  * Center: Lord Veera Venkata Satyanarayana Swamy
  * Right: Goddess Anantha Lakshmi
  * Left: Lord Shiva

### Unique Features

  * **Kalyana Mandapam:** Grand marriage hall (1000 vratham capacity)
  * **Sun Dial:** Displays accurate IST using astronomy
  * **Pampa River:** Encircles hill base
  * **460 Stone Steps:** Base to hilltop
  * **Motorable Road:** For elderly/disabled
