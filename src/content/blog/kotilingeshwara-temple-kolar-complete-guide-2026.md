## The Sacred History of Kotilingeshwara Temple

The Kotilingeshwara Temple has a relatively recent yet deeply spiritual origin story. In 1980, Swami Sambha Shiva Murthy had a divine vision to create a place where **one crore (10 million) Shivalingas** would be installed, symbolizing the infinite and omnipresent nature of Lord Shiva.

The first Shivalinga was consecrated on **October 10, 1980** , marking the beginning of this extraordinary spiritual project. Over the past four decades, the temple has grown into a massive complex spanning 15 acres, with over 90 lakh (9 million) Shivalingas already installed and counting.

### Why “Kotilingeshwara”?

In Kannada, “Koti” means crore (10 million), and “Lingeshwara” refers to Lord Shiva. The temple’s name reflects its ambitious goal of housing one crore Shivalingas, making it a unique pilgrimage destination in the world.

## Architectural Marvel: World’s Tallest Shivalinga

### Main Attractions

**1\. The 108-Feet Shivalinga** The centerpiece of the temple is the magnificent **108-feet-tall (33 meters) Shivalinga** , recognized as the world’s tallest. This towering structure stands as a testament to devotion and architectural brilliance, visible from kilometers away.

**2\. The 35-Feet Nandi Statue** Facing the main Shivalinga is an equally impressive **35-feet-tall (11 meters) Nandi statue** (Lord Shiva’s sacred bull), positioned on a grand platform. The sight of this massive Nandi in perfect alignment with the Shivalinga creates an awe-inspiring spiritual atmosphere.

**3\. Millions of Shivalingas** The temple grounds are adorned with millions of smaller Shivalingas arranged in neat rows across the 15-acre complex. Each linga has been installed by devotees as an offering of faith, with many bearing inscribed names of donors and their families.

### Sacred Trees

The temple complex houses two significant flowering trees:

  * **Nagalinga Tree** (Cannonball Tree) – Believed to fulfill wishes when devotees tie yellow threads around it
  * **Cannonball Flower Tree** – Known for its divine fragrance and medicinal properties

## Subsidiary Temples Within the Complex

The Kotilingeshwara Temple complex houses 11 smaller shrines dedicated to various deities:

  3. **Goddess Annapoorneshwari Temple**

This inclusive approach represents the unity of all Hindu deities under one spiritual umbrella.

### Linga Prathishta (Installation)

One of the most unique features of this temple is the opportunity for devotees to install their own Shivalinga. This sacred ritual involves:

  * **Selecting a Shivalinga** of desired size
  * **Payment** of ₹7,500 to ₹8,000 (varies by size)
  * **Inscription** of donor’s name on the linga
  * **Consecration ceremony** performed by temple priests
  * **Daily worship** of all installed lingas by the temple

This tradition allows devotees to establish a permanent spiritual connection with the temple and Lord Shiva.

## Major Festivals Celebrated at Kotilingeshwara Temple

### Maha Shivaratri (Grand Celebration)

**Timing:** February or March (based on lunar calendar)

The most significant festival at the temple, Maha Shivaratri attracts **lakhs of devotees** from across India and abroad. The celebration includes:

  * **All-night vigil** with continuous chanting
  * **Rudrabhisheka** – sacred abhishekam with milk, honey, ghee, and water
  * **Special lighting** and decorations throughout the complex
  * **Continuous darshan** opportunities for 24 hours
  * **Cultural programs** and devotional music

### Other Important Festivals

  * **Pradosha Vrata** – Special worship on the 13th day of lunar fortnight
  * **Karthika Deepotsava** – Festival of lights dedicated to Lord Shiva
  * **Navaratri** – Nine-day celebration honoring the Divine Mother
  * **Brahmarathotsava** – Grand chariot festival
  * **Karthika Masa** (November-December) – Entire month of special prayers
  * **Mondays** – Sacred day for Lord Shiva worship

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

### 1\. What are the Kotilingeshwara Temple timings in 2026?

The temple is open daily from **6:00 AM to 9:30 PM** without any break. Darshan is available throughout these hours.

### 2\. What is the entry fee for Kotilingeshwara Temple?

The entry ticket costs **₹20 per person**. Children under 14 years and physically challenged individuals get free entry during specific timings (9:00-10:00 AM and 7:30-9:00 PM).

### 3\. How far is Kotilingeshwara Temple from Bangalore?

The temple is approximately **97-100 km** from Bangalore city, which takes about 2 to 2.5 hours by road via NH75.

### 4\. What is special about Kotilingeshwara Temple?

Kotilingeshwara Temple houses the **world’s tallest Shivalinga at 108 feet (33 meters)** and has over **90 lakh (9 million) Shivalingas** installed by devotees. The temple aims to reach one crore (10 million) lingas.

### 5\. Can we install our own Shivalinga at the temple?

Yes, devotees can install their own Shivalinga through the **Linga Prathishta** ritual. The cost ranges from **₹7,500 to ₹8,000** depending on the size. Contact the temple counter for the procedure.

### 6\. When is Maha Shivaratri celebrated at Kotilingeshwara Temple?

Maha Shivaratri is typically celebrated in **February or March** based on the Hindu lunar calendar. It’s the biggest festival at the temple, attracting lakhs of devotees.

### 7\. Is there a dress code for visiting the temple?

Yes, traditional attire is recommended. **Men** should wear dhoti, kurta-pajama, or formal clothes. **Women** should wear sarees, salwar kameez, or half-sarees. Western casual wear like shorts and sleeveless tops are not allowed.

### 8\. Are there accommodation facilities at the temple?

Yes, the temple provides **basic guest rooms** for devotees. You can also find hotels and lodges in nearby Kolar town (35 km away). Prior booking is recommended during peak seasons.

### 9\. What is the best time to visit Kotilingeshwara Temple?

The best time is **October to March** when the weather is pleasant. However, visiting during **Maha Shivaratri** (February/March) offers a unique spiritual experience, though it’s very crowded.

### 10\. Is food available at the temple?

Yes, the temple offers **free meals (Anna Prasada)** during lunch (12:30-3:30 PM) and dinner (7:00-8:00 PM). Prasad is also distributed after aarti.

### 11\. How do I reach Kotilingeshwara Temple from Bangalore by bus?

Regular BMTC and KSRTC buses run from **Bangalore Majestic** and **K.R. Puram** to Kolar. From Kolar, take a local bus or auto to Kammasandra village where the temple is located.

### 12\. Can I take photographs inside the temple?

Yes, still photography is allowed with a **camera fee of ₹100**. However, video recording may be restricted in certain areas, and flash photography near deities is not permitted.

### 13\. What are the nearby tourist attractions?

Nearby attractions include **Antara Gange Caves** (40 km), **Chikka Tirupati** (63 km), **Someshwara Temple** in Mulbagal (27 km), and **Kolaramma Temple** in Kolar (35 km).

### 14\. Is the temple wheelchair accessible?

Yes, the temple has provisions for **wheelchair access** and ramps for elderly and physically challenged devotees. They also receive free darshan privileges.

### 15\. What festivals are celebrated at Kotilingeshwara Temple?

Major festivals include **Maha Shivaratri** , **Pradosha Vrata** , **Karthika Deepotsava** , **Navaratri** , and **Brahmarathotsava**. Mondays are also considered auspicious for Lord Shiva worship.

### 16\. Are there online booking facilities for darshan?

Currently, there is **no online booking** system for regular darshan. Tickets are available at the temple counter from 7:00 AM onwards on a first-come, first-served basis.

### 17\. How long does the darshan take?

During regular days, darshan takes approximately **30-45 minutes**. During festivals and weekends, it may take **1-2 hours** due to larger crowds.

### 18\. What is the significance of the Nandi statue at the temple?

The **35-feet-tall Nandi statue** facing the main Shivalinga represents Lord Shiva’s divine vehicle and symbolizes righteousness, strength, and devotion. It’s one of the tallest Nandi statues in India.

### 19\. Can I perform special poojas at the temple?

Yes, special poojas like **Rudrabhisheka** and **Abhishekam** can be performed. Contact the temple priests at the counter for arrangements and pricing.

### 20\. Is parking available at the temple?

Yes, the temple has a **dedicated parking area** with a nominal fee of **₹30**. The parking can accommodate both two-wheelers and four-wheelers.
