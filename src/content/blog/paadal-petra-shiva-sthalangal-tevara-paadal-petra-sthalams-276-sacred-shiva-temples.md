The Paadal Petra Shiva Sthalangal (also called Tevara Paadal Petra Sthalams or Thevara Sthalams) are the 276 sacred Shiva temples praised in the Tevaram hymns by the three great Nayanmars: Thirugnanasambandar, Thirunavukarasar (Appar), and Sundarar (7th–9th centuries CE). These hymns are foundational to Tamil Shaivism. The temples are primarily in Tamil Nadu (about 264–266), with a few in other regions like Andhra Pradesh, Karnataka, Kerala, Uttarakhand, Tibet, and Sri Lanka. The count is traditionally fixed at 276 — no changes in Shaivite canon or recent sources (2025–2026).

### Significance of Paadal Petra Shiva Sthalangal

These are divine abodes where the Nayanmars sang pathigams (hymns of praise) to Lord Shiva. Visiting them is highly auspicious for spiritual merit, liberation (moksha), and wish fulfillment. Key notes:

  * Chidambaram (Thillai) is often called the first (Koyil).
  * Kailash is the last (symbolic, not physically reachable for most).
  * Many are part of groups like Pancha Bhoota Sthalams (5 elements), Sapta Vidanga Sthalams, or Ashta Veerattana Sthalams.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

### 1\. What exactly are Paadal Petra Sthalangal?

**Paadal Petra Sthalangal** (பாடல் பெற்ற தலங்கள்) literally means “temples that received hymns.” These are 276 Shiva temples that were glorified in the Tevaram hymns composed by the three foremost Nayanar saints—**Thirugnana Sambandar, Appar, and Sundarar** —between the 6th and 8th centuries CE.

### 2\. Who composed the hymns for these temples?

The Tevaram hymns were composed by:

  * **Thirugnana Sambandar** (சம்பந்தர்) – Sang at many young age
  * **Appar** (அப்பர்) – Also known as Tirunavukkarasar
  * **Sundarar** (சுந்தரர்) – The last of the three

Together, their hymns form the first seven volumes of the Tirumurai, the canonical work of Tamil Shaiva Siddhanta.

### 3\. How are the 276 temples geographically distributed?

The temples are divided into five ancient regions:

  * **Thondai Nadu** : 32 temples (Chennai, Kanchipuram region)
  * **Nadunadu** : 22 temples (Villupuram, Cuddalore region)
  * **Chozha Nadu – North Cauvery** : 63 temples
  * **Chozha Nadu – South Cauvery** : 127 temples (largest concentration)
  * **Pandiya Nadu** : 14 temples (Madurai region)
  * **Andhra Pradesh** : 1 temple (Srikalahasti – included in Thondai Nadu list)

### 4\. Which is the only Paadal Petra Sthalam outside Tamil Nadu?

**Srikalahasti Temple** in Andhra Pradesh is the only Paadal Petra Sthalam located outside modern Tamil Nadu. It is included under Thondai Nadu in the traditional classification and was sung by Sundarar.

### 5\. What is the significance of the Cauvery river in this list?

The Chozha Nadu temples are divided into **Vadakarai** (north of Cauvery) and **Thenkarai** (south of Cauvery). The Cauvery delta region contains the highest concentration of Paadal Petra Sthalangal, with over 190 temples, making it the sacred heartland of Shaiva pilgrimage.

### 6\. Which are the “must-visit” Paadal Petra Sthalangal for first-time pilgrims?

For first-time pilgrims, these 10 temples are highly recommended:

  1. **Thillai Natarajar Temple** – Chidambaram
  2. **Arunachaleswarar Temple** – Thiruvannamalai
  3. **Brihadeeswarar Temple** – Thanjavur
  4. **Meenakshi Amman Temple** – Madurai
  5. **Ekambareswarar Temple** – Kanchipuram
  6. **Jambukeswarar Temple** – Thiruvanaikka (Trichy)
  7. **Kapaleeshwarar Temple** – Chennai (Mylapore)
  8. **Thyagaraja Temple** – Thiruvarur
  9. **Ramanathaswaram Temple** – Rameswaram
  10. **Srikalahasti Temple** – Srikalahasti (Andhra Pradesh)

### 7\. How long does it take to visit all 276 temples?

Visiting all 276 temples is a **lifetime pilgrimage**. Devotees typically undertake it in phases:

  * **Phase 1** : Chozha Nadu delta region – 30 to 45 days
  * **Phase 2** : Thondai Nadu and Nadunadu – 15 to 20 days
  * **Phase 3** : Pandiya Nadu – 10 to 15 days

Many pilgrims spread the journey over several years, visiting region by region.

### 8\. Are there any route maps available for planning the pilgrimage?

Yes! Detailed route maps for all 276 Paadal Petra Shiva Sthalangal are available. You can access comprehensive route maps and planning resources **[here](https://veludharan.blogspot.com/2014/12/paadal-petra-shiva-sthalangal_5.html)** to help organize your sacred journey.

### 9\. What is the best time of year to visit these temples?

The ideal time to visit Tamil Nadu for temple pilgrimage is from **November to February** when the weather is pleasant. The **Thai Poosam** (January/February) and **Maha Shivaratri** (February/March) festivals are especially auspicious times to visit many of these temples.

### 10\. Are there any apps or websites to help navigate this pilgrimage?

Several websites provide temple details, locations, and route maps. The [original source blog](https://veludharan.blogspot.com/2014/12/paadal-petra-shiva-sthalangal_5.html) remains one of the most comprehensive free resources. Additionally, Tamil Nadu Tourism website and various devotional apps now include GPS coordinates for many of these temples.

### Essential Tips:

  * **Start with the Chozha Nadu delta** – It has the highest concentration of temples
  * **Use Kumbakonam or Thiruvarur as base** – These towns are centrally located for the delta region
  * **Carry this list** – Mark off temples as you visit them
  * **Check temple timings** – Many temples close during afternoon hours (12:00 PM to 4:00 PM)
  * **Festival days** – Visiting during Brahmotsavam adds to the spiritual experience
