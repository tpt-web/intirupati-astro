The **Sri Prasanna Venkateswara Swamy Temple** in **Appalayagunta** , located 16 km from Tirupati, is a unique shrine where Lord Venkateswara is worshipped in the blissful **‘Abhaya Hasta’** (boon-giving) posture. This complete 2026 guide provides the exact temple timings, online booking steps, and all details for your visit.

## Temple Significance and History

This temple is revered as the place where Lord Venkateswara blessed sages during his marriage to Goddess Padmavathi. Built in **1232 AD** and managed by TTD since 1988, the main deity’s posture is believed to quickly fulfill devotees’ wishes. The temple also houses shrines to Goddess Padmavathi, Andal, and Lord Anjaneya.

## [How to Book Sevas Online](/blog/ttd-online-booking-guide-darshan-sevas-rooms-accommodation/) Process

All sevas must be booked in advance via the official TTD website.

  1. Visit the TTD booking portal: **[ttdevasthanams.ap.gov.in](https://ttdevasthanams.ap.gov.in/)**
  2. Log in with your TTD user ID or create a new account.
  3. Navigate to “Seva Booking” and select “Sri Prasanna Venkateswara Swamy Temple, Appalayagunta”.
  4. Choose your desired seva, date, and time slot.
  5. Fill in devotee details and complete the online payment.  
**Important Tip:** Book Kalyanotsavam and other special sevas well in advance, as slots are limited and fill quickly.

## How to Reach the Temple

  * **From Tirupati:** Appalayagunta is 16 km away, about a 30-40 minute drive.
  * **Transport:** Regular TTD buses, private taxis, and auto-rickshaws are available from Tirupati.
  * **Best Time to Visit:** For a peaceful experience, visit on weekdays. To witness special rituals, plan your visit for a Friday (Abhishekam) or the monthly Sravana Nakshatram day (Kalyanotsavam).

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: What is unique about the Appalayagunta deity?**  
A: The main deity is Sri Prasanna Venkateswara Swamy in the rare and granting **‘Abhaya Hasta’** posture.

**Q: Can I book sevas on the spot at the temple?**  
A: No. All sevas must be booked online in advance through the official TTD portal.

**Q: Is there a dress code for the temple?**  
A: Yes, modest traditional attire is recommended. Avoid shorts and sleeveless tops.

**Q: What are the free darshan timings?**  
A: Free Sarvadarshanam is available during the slots listed in the daily schedule table.

**Q: Are there accommodation facilities nearby?**  
A: Limited guest houses exist near the temple. Most pilgrims stay in Tirupati, which offers a wide range of TTD and private accommodations.
