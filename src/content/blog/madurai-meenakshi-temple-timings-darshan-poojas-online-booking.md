The **Meenakshi Amman Temple** in Madurai stands as one of India’s greatest architectural and spiritual wonders. This historic temple dedicated to Goddess Meenakshi (Parvati) and Lord Sundareswarar (Shiva) follows a precise daily schedule of rituals that has been maintained for centuries. Understanding the temple timings is essential for planning a meaningful visit, whether you seek peaceful darshan or wish to witness its vibrant ceremonies.

**Key Information at a Glance:**

  * **📍 Location:** Madurai, Tamil Nadu
  * **🕒 Open All Days:** 365 days a year
  * **🎫 Entry:** Free for general darshan (special darshan tickets available)
  * **⭐ Best For:** Architecture, spirituality, cultural experience, photography

## Madurai Meenakshi Temple Daily Timings 2025

The temple follows a two-session opening schedule with specific timings for different types of visits.

### Regular Opening Hours for Darshan

Session| Time| Experience Highlights  
---|---|---  
**Morning Darshan**|  5:00 AM – 12:30 PM| Less crowded from 5-7 AM; ideal for peaceful worship  
**Evening Darshan**|  4:00 PM – 10:00 PM| Lamp-lit corridors; beautiful sunset ambiance  
  
**Important Notes:**

  * The temple may briefly close around midday for ritual preparations
  * **Last entry** is typically 30-45 minutes before closing time
  * Evening photography may have restrictions
  * Timings may vary by 15-30 minutes on special days

## Daily Pooja Schedule & Ceremony Timings

Witnessing the temple’s six daily ceremonies (poojas) offers a deep insight into ancient traditions maintained for centuries.

### Morning Poojas

Pooja Name| Time| Ritual Significance  
---|---|---  
**Thiruvanandal Pooja**|  5:00 AM – 5:45 AM| Deities bathed and dressed; morning Aarti performed  
**Vilaa Pooja**|  8:00 AM – 9:15 AM| Morning ablutions; deities decorated with fresh flowers  
  
### Midday & Evening Poojas

Pooja Name| Time| Ritual Significance  
---|---|---  
**Kalasandhi Pooja**|  10:30 AM – 11:20 AM| Elaborate midday rituals; symbolic resting of deities  
**Maalai Pooja**|  4:30 PM – 5:15 PM| Reopening ceremony with music and fresh offerings  
**Ardhajama Pooja**|  7:30 PM – 8:15 PM| Evening worship with oil lamps and special offerings  
**Palliyarai Pooja**|  9:30 PM – 10:30 PM| Final ceremony; deities ritually put to sleep  
  
_Note: All pooja times are approximate and may vary slightly. Special ceremonies on certain days can alter this schedule._

## Special Festival Timings & Events

The temple’s schedule expands during major festivals, offering extended hours and unique experiences.

### Major Annual Festivals

Festival| Period| Special Timings & Highlights  
---|---|---  
**Chithirai Festival**|  April-May| 12-day festival; **4:00 AM – 12:00 AM** hours; celestial wedding celebration  
**Float Festival**|  January-February| Until **11:00 PM** ; deities on decorated floats at Mariamman Teppakulam tank  
**Avani Moola Festival**|  August-September| 10-day festival; **4:00 AM** start; evening processions until **10:00 PM**  
  
### Monthly Special Days

  * **Pradosham Days** (twice monthly): Special evening rituals until **10:00 PM**
  * **Full Moon Days** : Additional ceremonies at **7:00 PM** with special bath rituals

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q1: What are the Madurai Meenakshi Temple timings for tomorrow?**  
A: The temple generally follows the standard schedule (5:00 AM-12:30 PM & 4:00 PM-10:00 PM) unless it’s a special festival day. Check the temple notice board or website for last-minute changes.

**Q2: How can I book Meenakshi Temple darshan tickets online?**  
A: Visit the official Tamil Nadu Temple Administration website or authorized third-party portals. Online booking is available for special darshan, VIP darshan, and abhishekam tickets.

**Q3: What is the duration of darshan at Meenakshi Temple?**  
A: General darshan typically takes 1-2 hours depending on crowd levels. Special darshan tickets reduce this to 30-45 minutes. Festival days may involve longer waiting times.

**Q4: Is photography allowed inside Meenakshi Temple?**  
A: Photography is permitted in outer corridors and courtyards but restricted in inner sanctums and during certain ceremonies. Mobile phones must be switched off in prayer areas.

**Q5: What is the best time to avoid crowds at Meenakshi Temple?**  
A: Weekday mornings between 5:00-7:00 AM offer the least crowded experience. The months of June, July, September, and October (excluding festivals) also see fewer visitors.

**Q6: How far is Madurai Meenakshi Temple from the railway station?**  
A: The temple is approximately 2 kilometers from Madurai Junction Railway Station, about a 10-minute drive or 25-minute walk.

**Q7: Are there accommodation facilities near the temple?**  
A: Yes, Madurai offers numerous accommodation options ranging from budget lodges to luxury hotels within walking distance of the temple complex.
