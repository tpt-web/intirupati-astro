Seeking the cosmic dance of Lord Shiva as Nataraja? **Thillai Nataraja Temple** (also called Chidambaram Nataraja Temple) in Chidambaram, Tamil Nadu, is one of the Pancha Bhoota Sthalams representing the element of **Akasha (Space/Ether)**. This ancient Chola-era temple is world-famous for the **Chidambara Rahasya** (secret of Chidambaram) — an empty space symbolizing the formless divine, Lord Nataraja’s cosmic dance, exquisite Bharatanatyam carvings on pillars, and vibrant rituals. It’s a spiritual powerhouse for devotees seeking grace in arts, knowledge, liberation, and obstacle removal.

### What is Thillai Nataraja Temple?

Dedicated to Lord Shiva as Nataraja (Cosmic Dancer) and Goddess Sivakami (Parvati), the temple features the iconic Nataraja idol with raised foot, surrounded by a halo of flames. Key highlights include the **Ponnambalam** (Golden Hall), **Chit Sabha** (Hall of Consciousness with Chidambara Rahasya), 1,000-pillared hall with dance poses, and five courtyards. It’s managed by the temple trust (Dikshitars) and draws crowds for its unique Shaivite traditions.

### Benefits of Visiting

  * Removal of doshas and life hurdles through Nataraja’s grace
  * Blessings for excellence in dance, music, arts, and knowledge
  * Spiritual enlightenment and moksha (liberation)
  * Peace of mind and protection from negative energies
  * Special grace on **Arudra Darisanam** (full moon in Margazhi, Dec-Jan) and festivals

Best during Margazhi (Dec-Jan) for Arudra festival or Maha Shivaratri.

### Darshan Timings (Daily)

Temple operates with a midday break; timings consistent year-round:

  * **Morning Darshan** : 6:00 AM – 12:00 PM (Noon)
  * **Afternoon Break** : 12:00 PM – 5:00 PM (Sanctum closed)
  * **Evening Darshan** : 5:00 PM – 10:00 PM
  * **Key Poojas** (approximate): 
    * Kalasandhi Pooja: 7:45 AM – 9:00 AM
    * Uchikalam: Around 10:00 AM – 11:00 AM
    * Saayarakshai: 5:15 PM – 6:00 PM
    * Ardhajaamam (Night Pooja with Abhishekam): 9:00 PM – 10:00 PM
  * Temple fully closes around 10:00 PM after final aarti.

Report early for morning/evening slots; queues longer on Fridays, weekends, and festivals.

### Entry Fee and Special Darshan

  * **General Darshan** : Free (queues 30 min–2+ hours)
  * **Special Darshan** : Rs. 100 (faster access; available at counters)
  * No major paid VIP like Tirupati; some sevas offline.

### Sevas and Poojas

Major sevas include Abhishekam (Spatika Linga, Ruby Nataraja), Archana, and daily rituals.

  * **Abhishekam Seva** : Rs. 72,000 per family (elaborate, includes offerings/prasad; offline booking at temple counter or trust office; slots morning/evening around 7:00 AM or 6:30 PM)
  * Other archana/poojas: Lower cost (Rs. 100–500+); inquire onsite.
  * **Online Booking** : Limited/no official full online for main sevas; some third-party/virtual via sites like epuja.co.in or divinvite.com (e.g., special archana Rs. 1,000+ with prasadam delivery). Official is mostly offline — register at East Gopuram counter or trust office.
  * Helpline: +91 94436 35280 or check [www.chidambaramnataraja.org](http://www.chidambaramnataraja.org/) (note: not fully official but reliable info).

### How to Reach Chidambaram

  * **By Road** : 235 km from Chennai (via ECR, 4–5 hours drive); 78 km from Pondicherry; well-connected by NH.
  * **By Bus** : Frequent TNSTC buses from Chennai, Trichy, Pondy; Chidambaram Bus Stand ~2 km from temple.
  * **By Train** : Chidambaram Railway Station (CDM) on Chennai-Mayiladuthurai line; major trains from Chennai/Trichy.
  * **By Air** : Nearest airport Puducherry (PNY ~70 km) or Chennai (MAA ~235 km).
  * **Local** : Auto/taxi from station/bus stand (Rs. 50–150); temple in town center.

### Accommodation & Facilities

  * Budget options: Arudhra Residency, Mansoor Lodge, Parisha Residency, Pari Lodge (near temple, Rs. 800–3,000).
  * Mid-range: Hotels in town or Pondicherry day trip.
  * Temple trust guesthouses limited; book private via apps (OYO/MakeMyTrip).
  * Free facilities: Cloak rooms (deposit mobiles/cameras — no photography inside); footwear storage at gates.
  * Tips: Eat South Indian meals nearby; avoid peak festival rush for stay.

### Important Rules & Tips

  * **Dress Code** : Traditional — Men: Dhoti/kurta; Women: Saree/salwar with dupatta (no jeans/T-shirts).
  * **Prohibitions** : No mobiles/cameras inside sanctum; no leather/footwear (remove outside); no non-veg/alcohol/smoking.
  * **Other Tips** : Arrive before 12 PM or after 5 PM; witness aarti/abhishekam; explore 1,000-pillared hall; combine with nearby Pichavaram Mangrove or Annamalai University; plan 4–5 hours for full visit.
  * **Best Time** : Early morning for peaceful darshan; festivals like Natyanjali (Feb-Mar) for dance performances.

This temple offers profound cosmic energy and artistic splendor.
