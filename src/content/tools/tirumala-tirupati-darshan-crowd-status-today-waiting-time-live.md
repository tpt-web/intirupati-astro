## Best Time to Visit Tirumala

To minimize wait times and have a peaceful darshan of Lord Venkateswara:

*   **Weekdays:** Tuesdays, Wednesdays, and Thursdays generally see much lower crowds than weekends.
*   **Early Morning (6 AM – 9 AM):** Shortest queues of the day.
*   **Avoid Festivals:** Brahmotsavams, Vaikuntha Ekadashi, and holiday weekends can push waiting times past 20+ hours.
*   **Monday / Friday:** Slightly higher rush due to weekly sevas.

## Essential Tips for Devotees

1.  **Dress Code:** Traditional dress is strictly mandatory. Men must wear a Dhoti, Kurta-Pyjama, or Pancha. Women must wear a Saree, Half-Saree, or Churidar with a dupatta. Jeans, T-shirts, shorts, and skirts are not allowed.
2.  **Aadhaar Card:** Carry your physical Aadhaar card. It is mandatory for collecting free SSD tokens and during reporting for online booked tickets.
3.  **Footwear Lockers:** Tirumala is footwear-free around the temple area. Utilize the TTD footwear deposit counters (now QR-code enabled) before entering the queue.
4.  **No Electronic Gadgets:** Mobile phones, cameras, laptops, and smartwatches are strictly prohibited inside the temple premises. Free deposit lockers are available at CRO and Vaikuntham queue complex entry.
