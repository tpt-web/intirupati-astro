Seeking the fierce yet protective blessings of Goddess Bhadrakali (Kodungallur Amma or Sri Kurumba Bhagavathy) for obstacle removal, victory over enemies, health, and prosperity? **Kodungallur Bhagavathy Temple** (Sree Kurumba Bhagavathy Temple) in Kodungallur, Thrissur district, Kerala, is one of Kerala’s most powerful Shakti Peethams and the head of 64 Bhadrakali temples. This ancient shrine is renowned for its intense energy, unique rituals like Guruthy (blood offerings in tradition), and the famous **Bharani Festival** — a vibrant, sometimes controversial celebration of devotion and purification. Managed by the temple trust (with some under Cochin Devaswom Board influence), it draws lakhs of devotees for its raw spiritual power.

### What is Kodungallur Bhagavathy Temple?

Dedicated to Goddess Bhadrakali in her fierce form, the temple features the deity as “Kodungallur Amma” (Mother of Kodungallur). It includes shrines for Shiva, Ganapathy, and others. The temple’s history links to ancient royal patronage (Cranganore kings) and legends of the goddess protecting the land. It’s famous for:

  * Guruthy Pooja (symbolic blood offerings)
  * Oracle (Velichappadu) dances
  * Nakshatra Pushpaanjali and other Vazhipadu

The Bharani Festival (March–April) is its highlight, involving rituals like Kozhikkallu Moodal (rooster sacrifice, symbolic now), Kavu Theendal (oracles entering the sanctum), and Therippattu songs.

### Benefits of Visiting

  * Removal of black magic, evil eyes, and life hurdles

Best during Bharani Festival or Tuesdays/Fridays for Devi.

### Bharani Festival Details (2026)

The intense annual festival (Meenam month, Bharani asterism) usually March–April:

  * **Main Bharani Day 2026** : Around Sunday, March 22, 2026 (core rituals; confirm exact via temple site closer to date).
  * **Duration** : Month-long festivities; peaks 7 days post-Bharani star.
  * **Key Rituals** : 
    * Kozhikkallu Moodal (symbolic cock sacrifice/start around mid-March, e.g., March 15, 2026 tentative).
    * Kavu Theendal (oracles storming sanctum).
    * Thalappoli processions, Revathi Vilakku (lamp lighting), Bharanippattu songs.
  * Massive crowds; high energy with oracles in trance.
  * Entry free; plan early arrival for darshan.

### How to Reach Kodungallur

  * **Location** : Pettumma, Kodungallur, Thrissur district, Kerala 680664.
  * **By Air** : Cochin International Airport (COK ~40–60 km); taxis/buses available.
  * **By Train** : Irinjalakuda Railway Station (~20–23 km) or Thrissur (~38 km); then bus/taxi.
  * **By Road** : Well-connected via NH-66; buses from Kochi (~45 km, 1–1.5 hours), Thrissur (~40 km), or Calicut.
  * **Local** : Auto/taxi from bus stand (~few km); temple central.

### Accommodation & Facilities

  * Limited temple stays; nearby private hotels/homestays (e.g., Vedic Village Retreat, Cherai Onetree Retreat, or budget options in Kodungallur town).
  * Book via MakeMyTrip/Expedia (Rs. 800+); high demand during Bharani — reserve early.
  * Free Annadanam/meals on festival days; cloak rooms for mobiles (no photography inside).

### Important Rules & Tips

  * Traditional attire mandatory; no modern wear.
  * Prohibitions: No mobiles/cameras in sanctum; no leather/footwear (remove outside); no non-veg/alcohol/smoking.
  * Festival crowds extreme — women/children careful; respect oracle traditions.
  * Combine with nearby Cherai Beach or Triprayar Temple.
  * High spiritual intensity — prepare mentally.
