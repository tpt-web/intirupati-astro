The Sri Venu Gopala Swamy Temple in Karvetinagaram is a major temple complex renowned for its beautiful deity of Lord Krishna playing the flute. Located in the Chittoor district, it is a significant pilgrimage site managed by the Tirumala Tirupati Devasthanams (TTD).

## Temple Significance and History

This temple is the **presiding deity of the Karvetinagar region** , with origins dating back over a thousand years to the Chola period. The main deity, Sri Venu Gopala Swamy, depicts Lord Krishna in a charming posture holding a flute (_Venu_), embodying divine joy and compassion. The temple is also historically important as the  _moolasthana_ (original seat) of the Karvetinagaram chieftains, who were ardent devotees and patrons.

## Major Sevas and Festivals

The temple’s ritual calendar includes daily, weekly, and annual celebrations.

Category| Event / Seva| Key Details  
---|---|---  
**Major Festival**| **Brahmotsavam**|  The most important annual festival, celebrated for 9 days in March/April with processions and special rituals.  
**Weekly Seva**| **Sahasra Kalasabhishekam**|  A grand abhishekam performed every Saturday, considered highly auspicious.  
**Other Festivals**| **Krishna Janmashtami, Vaikuntha Ekadasi**|  Celebrated with great devotion, featuring special pujas and decorations.  
  
## How to Reach the Temple

  * **Location:** The temple is located in the heart of **Karvetinagaram town** in the Chittoor district of Andhra Pradesh.
  * **From Tirupati:** Karvetinagaram is approximately **50 km northwest of Tirupati** , about a 1.5-hour drive by road.
  * **Transport:** Regular APSRTC buses and private taxis are available from Tirupati and nearby towns like Chittoor. The temple is well-connected by road.

## Visitor Information and Guidelines

  * **Sevas:** Common sevas like **Archana, Abhishekam, and Astottaram** are available. For details on specific sevas like the Saturday Sahasra Kalasabhishekam, devotees should inquire at the temple counter or check the official TTD website for booking procedures and donations.
  * **Dress Code:** Modest, traditional attire is recommended.
  * **Best Time to Visit:** The weather is pleasant from October to March. Visiting during the annual Brahmotsavam offers a spectacular experience, though it will be very crowded.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: Who is the main deity of this temple?**  
A: The main deity is Sri Venu Gopala Swamy, a form of Lord Krishna depicted holding a flute (_Venu_).

**Q: What is special about this temple?**  
A: It is the  _moolasthana_ (original seat) of the Karvetinagaram chieftains and is famous for its ancient deity and the grand Saturday  _Sahasra Kalasabhishekam_.

**Q: How far is it from Tirupati and how do I get there?**  
A: The temple is about 50 km from Tirupati, roughly a 1.5-hour drive. Regular buses and taxis are available.

**Q: Can I book sevas online?**  
A: While general darshan requires no booking, for specific sevas like Abhishekam, it is best to check the official TTD website (`ttdevasthanams.ap.gov.in`) or contact the temple administration for the latest booking procedures.

**Q: When is the best time to visit the temple?**  
A: For pleasant weather, visit between October and March. To witness the temple at its most vibrant, plan your visit during the annual Brahmotsavam in March/April.
