Facing health concerns, longevity issues, accidents, or fear of untimely death? **Maha Mrityunjaya Homam** (also called Mruthyunjaya Homam or Mrityunjaya Japam) at **Srisailam Temple** (Sri Mallikarjuna Swamy and Bhramaramba Devi Temple) is a powerful Vedic fire ritual dedicated to Lord Shiva as Mrityunjaya (Conqueror of Death). Performed at this Jyotirlinga and Shakti Peetham, it invokes the Maha Mrityunjaya Mantra for protection, healing, and long life. Srisailam, in Andhra Pradesh’s Nandyal district, is one of the most auspicious places for this homam due to its divine energy.

### What is Mrityunjaya Homam?

This homam involves sacred fire offerings, mantra chanting (108/1008 times or more), and invocations to Lord Shiva. It can be **Pratyaksha** (in-person) or **Paroksha** (virtual/online). The ritual lasts 45–90 minutes, with priests performing it on behalf of devotees. Ideal for health recovery, accident protection, overcoming Mrityu Dosha, or general well-being.

### Benefits of Mrityunjaya Homam

  * Conquest over untimely death and accidents
  * Longevity, good health, and recovery from illnesses
  * Removal of fears, negative energies, and doshas
  * Peace of mind, protection for family, and overall prosperity
  * Spiritual purification and divine grace from Shiva-Shakti

Many devotees perform it for elderly family members or after health scares.

### Cost and Packages

Costs vary by type (Pratyaksha vs. Paroksha) and inclusions:

  * **Pratyaksha (In-Person) Mrityunjaya Homam** : Rs. 1,500 (official temple rate for participation; some sources mention Rs. 508–527 earlier, but recent updates confirm Rs. 1,500).
  * **Paroksha (Virtual/Online) Mrityunjaya Homam** : Rs. 1,500 (performed remotely with video/prasadam courier).
  * Higher counts or elaborate Japam: Rs. 20,000–35,000+ via private priests (for intensive 1008+ japas).
  * **Allowed Persons** : Usually 1–2 (couple/family); confirm during booking. No additional materials needed; temple provides samagri.

### Timings and Schedule

  * **Pratyaksha Homam** : Daily around 6:30 AM–9:30 AM (often 7:30 AM or 8:30–9:30 AM slot; part of morning rituals).
  * **Paroksha Seva** : Performed as per daily schedule; virtual viewing available.
  * **Temple Timings** : Opens 4:30–5:00 AM; darshan 6:00 AM onward; homam in morning slots.
  * Performed **daily** ; best on Mondays, Pradosham, or Maha Shivaratri (Feb 2026 festival ongoing — check for special slots). Dress code: Traditional — men: dhoti/kurta; women: saree/salwar (no modern wear).

### Location

**Srisailam Temple** , Srisailam, Nandyal district, Andhra Pradesh (on Krishna River banks). Homam in temple premises (specific mandapam or near sanctum).

### How to Book Mrityunjaya Homam

**Pratyaksha (In-Person)** :

  1. Book online via official site: **srisailadevasthanam.org** → Pratyaksha Seva Booking.
  2. Select Mrityunjaya Homam, date, and pay.
  3. Or offline at temple donation/seva counter (arrive early).

**Paroksha (Virtual)** :

  1. Visit **srisailadevasthanam.org** → Paroksha Seva Booking (or aptemples.ap.gov.in).
  2. Register, select seva, enter details (names, gotra), upload ID proof (Aadhaar/PAN etc.).
  3. Pay online; receive confirmation/video link/prasadam details. Advance booking recommended (limited slots); no walk-in for Paroksha.

**Contact** : Srisaila Devasthanam: +91-8333901351 / +91-8524-287126; Email: [endow-eosri@gov.in](mailto:endow-eosri@gov.in).

### Important Rules and Tips

  * Upload valid ID for online booking.
  * Report early for in-person; fast till completion.
  * Prasadam couriered for Paroksha.
  * High demand during festivals (e.g., Maha Shivaratri Brahmotsavams Feb 2026).
  * Combine with Mallikarjuna-Bhramaramba darshan.
