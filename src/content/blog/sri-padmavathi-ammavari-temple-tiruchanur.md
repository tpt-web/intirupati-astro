Seeking the blessings of Goddess Padmavathi (consort of Lord Venkateswara) for wealth, prosperity, marital harmony, and removal of obstacles? **Sri Padmavathi Ammavari Temple** in Tiruchanur (also called Alamelumangapuram), about 5 km from Tirupati town, is a must-visit Shakti Peetham. Managed by **Tirumala Tirupati Devasthanams (TTD)** , it’s a serene spot where devotees often visit before or after Tirumala darshan to complete the spiritual circuit. The temple’s architecture, lotus pond, and divine atmosphere make it perfect for peaceful prayers.

### Why Visit Padmavathi Temple?

Goddess Padmavathi is revered as the source of all auspiciousness. Legend says Lord Venkateswara married Her here after penance. Visiting Tiruchanur first is traditional for many devotees. Key perks: Shorter queues than Tirumala, beautiful rituals, and famous kumkum archana for women seeking marital bliss.

### Types of Darshan Available

  * **Sarva Darshan (Free)** : Open to all; waiting time 30–90 minutes (longer on weekends/festivals).
  * **Special Entry Darshan (Rs. 200)** : Faster access (20–45 minutes); advance online booking monthly.
  * **Quick Darshan / Special Entrance** : Rs. 20–100 options for priority in some cases.
  * **Sevas** : Paid rituals like Suprabhatam, Sahasra Namarchana, Kumkum Archana, Padmavathi Parinayam (Kalyanotsavam).

Book only via official **TTD portal** — quotas release monthly (e.g., February 2026 Special Entry opened January 24, 2026, at 10:00 AM).

### Darshan & Seva Timings (Daily 2026)

Temple opens early; darshan almost throughout the day with short breaks:

  * **Temple Opens** : 5:00 AM (3:00–3:30 AM on Fridays for Suprabhatam)
  * **Suprabhatam** : 4:30–5:00 AM (earlier on Fridays)
  * **General Darshan (Sarva Darshan)** : 6:00 AM – 8:30 PM (with breaks ~12:00–1:00 PM or similar)
  * **Special Entry Slots** : Often 9:00 AM–6:00 PM (check portal for exact)
  * **Evening Darshan** : Up to 9:00–9:30 PM
  * **Closing** : 9:00–9:30 PM Key Sevas (book online):
  * Suprabhatam: Rs. 25–50 (1 person)
  * Sahasra Namarchana: Rs. 25–100
  * Kumkum Archana: Rs. 200 (2 persons, slots like 10–11 AM, 3–4 PM, 7–8 PM)
  * Padmavathi Parinayam (Kalyanotsavam): Rs. 500 (2 persons, 10:00–11:00 AM)
  * Ekantha Seva: Rs. 25–50 (night)

Best time: Early morning or evenings; Fridays auspicious for Devi.

### How to Book Darshan/Sevas Online in 2026

  1. Visit official portal: **[ttdevasthanams.ap.gov.in](http://ttdevasthanams.ap.gov.in/)** (or TTD app).
  2. Register/login with mobile/Aadhaar.
  3. Select “Temples” → Sri Padmavathi Ammavari Temple, Tiruchanoor.
  4. Choose Darshan/Seva, date, and slot (advance quotas monthly; e.g., next month opens ~last week of previous at 10:00 AM).
  5. Enter details (up to limited persons; ID required).
  6. Pay and download e-ticket (print/digital show at temple).

  * Special Entry Rs. 200: Monthly quota; book early as it sells fast.
  * Sevas: Limited per user; some lucky dip.
  * Current/same-day: Limited options at counters.

Always check live status on portal.

### How to Reach Tiruchanur

  * **From Tirupati** : 5 km; APSRTC buses (frequent, cheap), auto/taxi (Rs. 100–200), or private vehicle.
  * **From Tirumala** : ~25 km downhill; bus/taxi via Tirupati.
  * **Air/Rail** : Tirupati Airport/Station → Tiruchanur easy (10–15 min from town).
  * Parking: Available (free/paid); temple well-connected.

### Accommodation & Facilities

  * TTD guesthouses/cottages in Tirupati/Tiruchanur (book online via portal; affordable).
  * Private hotels/lodges in Tirupati (wide range).
  * Free Annadanam (meals) at temple.
  * Medical/first aid, cloak rooms (no mobiles/cameras inside; deposit at entrance).

### Important Rules & Tips

  * **Dress Code** : Traditional — Men: Dhoti/kurta; Women: Saree/salwar with dupatta (no jeans, shorts, modern wear).
  * **Prohibitions** : No mobiles/cameras inside sanctum; no non-veg/alcohol/smoking; bathe before entry.
  * **Other Tips** : Visit after head bath; combine with Tirumala trip (many do Padmavathi first); carry ID; avoid peak festivals for shorter waits; kumkum archana popular for women.
  * Prasadam: Available (laddus/vada in some sevas).

This temple offers calm, powerful blessings from Goddess Padmavathi.
