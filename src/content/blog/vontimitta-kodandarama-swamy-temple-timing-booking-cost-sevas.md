## **Vontimitta Temple: Where Ramayana Echoes in Stone**

Nestled in the heart of Andhra Pradesh’s cultural landscape, the **Vontimitta Kodandarama Swamy Temple** stands as a serene yet powerful testament to the Ramayana’s legacy. Unlike the bustling  _gopurams_ of Tirupati, this temple offers a uniquely peaceful and historically rich experience, centered on the divine trio of Lord Rama, Lakshmana, and Goddess Sita.

This 16th-century architectural marvel is not just a place of worship but a chapter from the epic brought to life, making it a **must-visit spiritual detour** on the Tirupati-Chennai route.

## **What Makes Vontimitta Temple Special?**

✅ **Unique Idol Configuration:** The temple enshrines a rare standing posture of **Lord Rama with a bow (Kodanda)** , flanked by **Lakshmana and Sita** —all three deities in a single sanctum.  
✅ **No Hanuman Idol:** Legend says Hanuman stands guard  _outside_ , making this one of the few Rama temples without an idol of Hanuman inside.  
✅ **Architectural Gem:** Built in the **Vijayanagara style** , featuring a grand  _mandapam_ with 32 pillars, renowned for its acoustics and sculptural beauty.  
✅ **Cultural Significance:** Believed to be the site where the poet **Bammera Potana** translated the Bhagavata Purana into Telugu.

### ✅ How to Book Sevas Online via TTD Website

The process is Simple, but **availability is limited**.

  1. **Visit the Portal** : Go to the official TTD Seva Booking portal: [https://tirupatibalaji.ap.gov.in](https://tirupatibalaji.ap.gov.in/).
  2. **Select the Temple** : After login, navigate to “Seva Booking” and select **“Sri Kodanda Rama Swamy Devasthanam, VONTIMITTA”** from the list of temples.
  3. **Check Availability** : Select your desired date and view the available seva list. The **weekly and monthly sevas** (Abhisekham/Saturday, Swarna Pushparchana/Sunday, Kalyanotsavam/Pournami) will appear here **if slots are open** for booking.
  4. **Complete Booking** : Select the seva, fill in devotee details, and proceed with online payment.

### ⚠️ Important Booking Notes

Seva| Key Booking Consideration  
---|---  
**Abhisekham (Sat)** & **Swarna Pushparchana (Sun)**|  Slots are very limited (often **1 person** per day). Booking opens **months in advance** and gets filled quickly.

### 🔍 What If Online Booking is Full?

If the online portal shows no availability, you still have options:

  * **Offline/Ration Quota** : A portion of tickets is held for **offline sale at the temple**. You must inquire at the **Vontimitta temple counter** well in advance (often the previous day or early morning).
  * **Cancellation Slots** : Keep checking the online portal periodically, as cancelled tickets are re-released.

## **The Legend of “Vontimitta”: A Story of Devotion**

The temple’s name originates from a fascinating legend. Two devout devotees, **Vontudu and Mittudu** , wished to build a temple for Lord Rama. Miraculously, the idol of Rama appeared before them, fulfilling their wish. Moved by their unwavering devotion, Rama declared the place would be known by their combined names—**Vontimitta**.

This story underscores the temple’s core theme: **pure bhakti rewarded by divine grace.**

### 📍 Key Historical & Travel Information at a Glance

  * **Significance & Name**: Built during the **Vijayanagara Empire** (16th century) and known as **“Ekasilanagaram”** because the main idols of Rama, Sita, and Lakshmana are carved from a **single stone**.
  * **Unique Feature** : Lord Hanuman does **not** have an idol inside the main sanctum. A separate shrine for him is located outside the main temple complex.
  * **Major Festival** : **Sri Rama Navami** is the main festival, with the unique tradition of performing **Sita Rama Kalyanam (celestial wedding)** on the night of  _Chaturdasi_ (a day before full moon) instead of at noon.
  * **How to Reach** : 
    * **Nearest Town/City** : **Kadapa** (~25-30 km away).
    * **Nearest Railway Station** : **Vontimitta** (1.5 km) or **Kadapa Junction** (26 km).
    * **Nearest Airport** : **Kadapa Airport** (30 km) or **Tirupati Airport** (113 km).
  * **Accommodation & Food**: **Free Annadanam** (lunch) is served daily between **11:00 AM and 3:00 PM**. Pilgrims can find accommodation in **common halls** within Vontimitta or stay in **Kadapa** , which offers more lodging options.

## Frequently Asked Questions (**FAQs):**

**Q1: What is the significance of the Kodandarama Swamy Temple?**  
**A1:** The temple is historically and spiritually significant for its unique idol setup (Rama, Lakshmana, Sita together), its connection to poet Potana, and its embodiment of a key Ramayana site in Andhra.

**Q2: How far is Vontimitta from Tirupati?**  
**A2:** Vontimitta is about **130-140 km from Tirupati** (approx. 3-3.5 hours drive). It is closer to Kadapa (25 km) and makes for an excellent stop if traveling between Chennai and Tirupati.

**Q3: Are there any accommodation facilities near the temple?**  
**A3:** While basic lodgings are available in Vontimitta, for more comfortable stays, **Kadapa** city offers a wider range of hotels and guest houses.

**Q4: What are the major festivals celebrated here?**  
**A4:** **Sri Rama Navami** is celebrated with immense fervor, featuring  _Sita Rama Kalyanam_ (celestial wedding). Other festivals include **Vaikunta Ekadasi** and **Karthika Deepam**.

**Q5: Why is there no Hanuman idol inside the temple?**  
**A5:** As per legend, Lord Rama instructed Hanuman to guard the temple entrance. Devotees believe Hanuman’s presence is so powerful at the gate that an inner idol is unnecessary.

**Q6: Is photography allowed inside the temple?**  
**A6:** Photography is generally restricted inside the main sanctum and  _mandapam_. It’s best to ask for permission from the temple authorities upon arrival.

**Q7: Which sevas at Vontimitta can be booked online?**  
A1: Only **weekly and monthly sevas** can be booked online via the TTD portal: **Abhisekham (Saturday)** , **Swarna Pushparchana (Sunday)** , and **Kalyanotsavam (on Pournami day)**. Daily sevas like Archana or Antharalaya Darshan are **offline only**.

**Q8: Can I book accommodation at Vontimitta Temple online?**  
A2: **No.** The TTD portal does not offer online room booking for Vontimitta. Pilgrims typically use the common halls at the temple or stay in hotels in **Kadapa town** (25-30 km away).

**Q9: How do I book the daily Archana or Antharalaya Darshan?**  
A3: These are booked **offline**. Visit the temple’s **Seva/Administration counter** on the day of your visit to check availability and purchase a ticket.

**Q10: What is the temple’s contact number for inquiries?**  
A4: You can call the temple office at **📞 08562-244333** to verify darshan timings, inquire about offline seva availability, or ask other questions.

**Q11: How far in advance do online seva bookings open?**  
A5: TTD typically releases seva tickets **90 days (3 months) in advance**. Slots for popular sevas like Kalyanotsavam sell out very quickly, so it’s best to book as soon as the calendar opens.

**Q12: Is photography allowed inside the temple?**  
A6: Photography is generally **restricted inside the main sanctum (garbha griha) and the inner mandapam**. It’s best to ask for explicit permission from the temple authorities or security personnel upon arrival. You may be allowed to take photos in the outer courtyards.

**Q13: What if the online portal shows “No Seats Available” for a seva?**  
A7: A portion of tickets is often reserved for **offline sale at the temple counter**. Go to the counter **a day in advance or early in the morning** on the day of the seva to check for these “ration quota” tickets.

**Q14: What is the official TTD booking website?**  
A8: The main portal for seva and darshan bookings for TTD-managed temples is: **[https://tirupatibalaji.ap.gov.in](https://tirupatibalaji.ap.gov.in/)**.

**Q15: Are there different rules during major festivals like Sri Rama Navami?**  
A9: Yes. During **Sri Rama Navami** , special darshan rules, crowded conditions, and altered seva schedules apply. Always check the **“News & Announcements”** section on the TTD website or contact the temple directly before planning a festival visit.
