Two main free darshan systems in Tirupati—Slotted Sarva Darshan (SSD) and Divya Darshan (DD). The processes for these are distinct, so I’ve broken them down clearly.

Here is a summary of the two main free darshan options to help you plan for Tirumala Darshan:

Feature| **Slotted Sarva Darshan (SSD)**| **Divya Darshan (DD)**  
---|---|---  
**Primary Purpose**|  General free darshan for all pilgrims.| Reward for pilgrims who trek the footpaths to Tirumala.  
**How to Obtain**|  Offline tokens from TTD counters in **Tirupati**.| Offline tokens at the **start points of the footpaths** (Alipiri or Srivari Mettu).  
**Key Requirement**|  Aadhaar card for each adult. No specific travel mode required.| **Must trek** the corresponding footpath (Alipiri or Srivari Mettu) to validate the token.  
**Typical Timing**|  Counters open early (5 AM or 9 AM); tokens for same-day or next-day darshan.| Counters open at 4 AM (Alipiri) or 5 AM (Srivari Mettu). Tokens are for same-day use.  
**Best For**|  Devotees traveling by bus, car, or other transport to Tirumala.| Devotees undertaking the traditional pilgrimage by foot.  
  
### Slotted Sarva Darshan (SSD)

SSD is the standard free darshan system designed to manage crowds with a time slot, significantly reducing wait times.

  * **Where to Get Tokens** : Tokens are issued **only offline** at specific TTD complexes in Tirupati: 
    * **[Srinivasam Complex](/srinivasam-complex-accommodation-prices-booking-complete-guide/)** (opposite the main bus stand)
    * **[Vishnu Nivasam](/vishnu-nivasam-accommodation-room-booking-prices-complete-details/)** (opposite the railway station)
    * **Govindaraja Swamy Choultry** (behind the railway station)
  * **Process & Timings**: Tokens are issued on a **first-come, first-served** basis until the daily quota (reportedly 10,000-15,000) is exhausted. Counter timings may start as early as **5:00 AM** for same-day darshan or around **9:00 AM** for next-day darshan. **Always arrive very early**.
  * **What You Need** : A physical **Aadhaar card** is mandatory for each adult. Children under 12 do not need a separate token.
  * **After Getting the Token** : Report to the **ATGH Circle** in Tirumala at the time printed on your token for darshan.

### Divya Darshan (DD) for Footpath Trekkers

Divya Darshan is a **free darshan privilege specifically for pilgrims who walk to Tirumala** via the sacred footpaths.

  * **Two Paths, Two Counters** : You must collect your token at the starting point of the path you choose to walk: 
    * **Alipiri Mettu Path** (3,550 steps, ~9 km): Tokens are issued at the **Bhudevi Complex** near the Alipiri bus stand. The counter opens around **4:00 AM**.
    * **Srivari Mettu Path** (2,550 steps, ~2.1 km): Tokens are issued at the **1240th step** on this path. The counter opens around **5:00 AM**.
  * **The Crucial Rule** : This is the most important point. **The DD token is only valid if you complete the trek on the same path where you collected it.** For Alipiri, you must scan your token at the **2083rd step**. Taking a bus or another route will invalidate your token.
  * **What You Need** : Any government-issued **photo ID** (Aadhaar is commonly used) for adults. Children under 12 need ID but do not get a separate token.

### 📋 Your Essential Pilgrim Checklist

To avoid confusion and disappointment, keep these key points in mind:

  1. **Choose Your Path First** : Decide if you will travel by vehicle (get **SSD**) or by foot (get **DD**). They are completely different systems.
  2. **Carry Original ID** : **Aadhaar is mandatory for SSD** and highly recommended for DD. Carry originals for all adults.
  3. **Arrive Extremely Early** : For both SSD and DD tokens, queues form hours before counters open, especially on weekends and holidays. Plan accordingly.
  4. **Verify Official Info** : Token systems and timings can change. Always confirm the latest rules by calling the **TTD Helpline at 155257** or checking official TTD announcements.

### 🗺️ How to Plan Your Darshan

Your choice between **SSD** and **DD** depends on your pilgrimage plan:

  * **Option A: I will travel to Tirumala by bus or car.**  
Go to an **SSD counter** in Tirupati (Srinivasam/Vishnu Nivasam), get your token with your Aadhaar, then proceed to Tirumala by road and report at your allotted time.
  * **Option B: I will trek the Alipiri footpath.**  
Go to **Bhudevi Complex** at Alipiri very early in the morning, get your **DD token** , and then **start your walk** , ensuring you scan your token at the 2083rd step.
  * **Option C: I will trek the Srivari Mettu footpath.**  
Go directly to the **Srivari Mettu starting point** , collect your **DD token at the 1240th step** , and then continue your walk to Tirumala.
